01

Definitions

In this agreement, the following terms have the meanings set out below:

"Articles" The standard model articles of association of Groomr Ltd as filed with Companies House on incorporation, as may be amended from time to time in accordance with the Companies Act 2006.
"Board" The board of directors of Groomr Ltd from time to time.
"Business" The operation of a two-sided dog grooming marketplace connecting dog owners with independent professional groomers, and any related activities carried on by the Company.
"Completion" The date on which the Investor's subscription monies are received by the Company and the Shares are allotted and issued.
"Drag-Along Right" The right of the Founder, as majority shareholder, to require the Investor to sell their Shares on the same terms in the event of a sale of the Company, as further described in Clause 7.
"Ordinary Shares" Ordinary shares of £[nominal value] each in the capital of the Company, carrying full voting rights, the right to participate in dividends, and the right to participate in any distribution on a winding up, in each case on a pro-rata basis with all other ordinary shares.
"SEIS" The Seed Enterprise Investment Scheme established under Part 5A of the Income Tax Act 2007, as amended.
"Shares" The [Number of shares] ordinary shares to be issued to the Investor at Completion, representing 5% of the issued share capital of the Company immediately following Completion.
"Tag-Along Right" The right of the Investor to participate in any sale of shares by the Founder on the same terms, as further described in Clause 8.
"Transfer Notice" A written notice given by a shareholder wishing to transfer any Shares, as described in Clause 6.

02

Subscription for shares

2.1
Subscription

Subject to the terms of this agreement, the Investor agrees to subscribe for, and the Company agrees to allot and issue to the Investor, the Shares at Completion in consideration of the payment of £10,000 (the "Subscription Price").

2.2
Nature of shares

The Shares are full-risk ordinary shares. They are not redeemable, carry no special rights to the assets of the Company, and confer no right to require the Company or any other person to purchase or redeem them at any time. The Investor acknowledges that the value of the Shares may go down as well as up and that the entire Subscription Price may be lost.

2.3
Completion
(a)Completion shall take place on the date agreed between the Parties, provided that the SEIS Advance Assurance confirmation from HMRC has been received by the Company.
(b)At Completion, the Investor shall pay the Subscription Price to the Company by bank transfer.
(c)Upon receipt of the Subscription Price, the Company shall allot and issue the Shares to the Investor, update the register of members accordingly, and file the necessary return with Companies House within the period required by law.
2.4
SEIS compliance

The Company shall use reasonable endeavours to ensure that the Shares qualify for SEIS relief and shall, as soon as practicable following Completion, submit to HMRC the compliance statement required to obtain the SEIS1 certificate. The Company shall provide the Investor with the SEIS1 certificate promptly upon receipt. The Company does not warrant that SEIS relief will be available and the Investor is advised to take independent tax advice.

03

Rights of the investor

3.1
Voting

The Shares carry the right to vote at general meetings of the Company on a one-vote-per-share basis, in proportion to all other ordinary shares in issue.

3.2
Dividends

The Investor shall be entitled to receive dividends declared by the Company in respect of the Shares, on a pro-rata basis with all other holders of ordinary shares. The declaration of dividends is at the sole discretion of the Board and no dividend shall be payable unless the Company has sufficient distributable reserves. There is no obligation on the Company to declare any dividend.

3.3
Information rights

The Company shall provide the Investor with written updates on the trading performance of the Business on a quarterly basis, covering at minimum: active groomers on the platform, booking volumes, revenue, and material developments affecting the Company's business or financial position. The Company shall also notify the Investor promptly of any matter that would constitute a material change to the Company's business, including any proposed fundraising, restructuring, or change to the Articles.

3.4
Pre-emption on new shares

If the Company proposes to issue new shares, the Investor shall have the right to subscribe for such number of new shares as would maintain their proportional shareholding, on the same terms and at the same price as offered to other subscribers, subject to such rights being waived or disapplied in accordance with the Articles and applicable law. This right does not apply to shares issued under any employee share scheme or to shares issued as consideration for an acquisition.

3.5
Winding up

In the event of a winding up or return of capital, the Investor shall be entitled to participate in any distribution of assets on a pro-rata basis with all other holders of ordinary shares, after satisfaction of the Company's liabilities. There is no preference or priority attaching to the Shares.

04

Obligations of the founder

4.1
Conduct of business

The Founder shall use reasonable endeavours to carry on the Business in a proper and efficient manner and in accordance with good business practice, with the objective of growing the Company's revenues, customer base, and commercial position over the long term.

4.2
Use of investment proceeds

The Founder confirms that the Subscription Price shall be used solely for the qualifying trading activities of the Company as set out in the Use of Investment Funds document included in the investment pack, and shall be deployed within three years of Completion in accordance with SEIS requirements.

4.3
Reserved matters

The following actions shall require the prior written consent of the Investor for so long as they hold not less than 3% of the issued share capital of the Company:

(a)Any change to the nature of the Business that would fundamentally alter its qualifying trade for SEIS purposes.
(b)Any winding up or dissolution of the Company other than for the purpose of a solvent reconstruction.
(c)Any amendment to the Articles that would materially and adversely affect the rights attaching to the Shares.

05

Transfer of shares

5.1
General restriction

No shareholder may transfer any Shares except in accordance with the provisions of this Clause 5 and the Articles. Any purported transfer in breach of this Clause shall be void and of no effect.

5.2
Transfer notice

A shareholder wishing to transfer any Shares (the "Transferor") must first give a Transfer Notice to the Company and all other shareholders specifying: (a) the number of Shares to be transferred; (b) the proposed transferee; and (c) the proposed transfer price.

5.3
Board approval

No transfer of Shares shall be registered without the prior written approval of the Board. The Board may withhold approval in its absolute discretion, provided that it shall not withhold approval unreasonably where the proposed transferee is a person of good standing and financial standing reasonably acceptable to the Board.

5.4
Right of first refusal
(a)Upon receipt of a Transfer Notice, the other shareholders shall have the right to purchase the Shares proposed to be transferred at the price specified in the Transfer Notice (the "Offer Price").
(b)The Founder shall have the first right of refusal to acquire all (but not part only) of the Shares specified in the Transfer Notice at the Offer Price, exercisable within 20 Business Days of receipt of the Transfer Notice.
(c)If the Founder does not exercise this right within the 20 Business Day period, the Transferor may proceed with the proposed transfer to the named transferee at no less than the Offer Price, subject to Board approval under Clause 5.3.
5.5
SEIS holding period

The Investor acknowledges that disposing of the Shares within three years of Completion may result in the withdrawal or clawback of SEIS income tax relief. The Investor is solely responsible for any such tax consequences and is advised to seek independent tax advice before any transfer.

06

Drag-along rights

6.1
Drag-along trigger

If the Founder (or any group of shareholders holding in aggregate not less than 75% of the issued share capital) receives a bona fide offer from a third party to acquire 100% of the issued share capital of the Company (a "Sale"), the Founder may by written notice (a "Drag Notice") require the Investor to sell all of their Shares as part of that Sale.

6.2
Terms of drag-along
(a)The Investor shall sell their Shares on the same terms and at the same price per share as those applicable to the Founder in the Sale.
(b)The Drag Notice shall specify the identity of the proposed buyer, the price per share, and the proposed completion date of the Sale.
(c)The Investor shall execute all documents and take all steps reasonably required to complete the Sale promptly following receipt of the Drag Notice.
6.3
Protection of investor

The Founder shall not exercise the Drag-Along Right unless the consideration payable to the Investor per Share is not less than the consideration payable to the Founder per Share, and the Sale is on arm's length commercial terms to an unconnected third party.

07

Tag-along rights

7.1
Tag-along trigger

If the Founder proposes to transfer any Shares to a third party (other than a permitted transfer under Clause 5), the Investor shall have the right (but not the obligation) to require the Founder to procure that the proposed buyer also acquires all (but not part only) of the Investor's Shares as part of the same transaction (the "Tag-Along Right").

7.2
Exercise of tag-along
(a)The Founder shall notify the Investor in writing of any proposed transfer, specifying the proposed buyer, price per share, and terms of the transaction (a "Tag Notice").
(b)The Investor shall have 15 Business Days from receipt of the Tag Notice to notify the Founder in writing that they wish to exercise the Tag-Along Right.
(c)If the Investor exercises the Tag-Along Right, the Founder shall not complete the proposed transfer unless the proposed buyer also acquires the Investor's Shares at the same price per share and on the same terms.
7.3
Non-exercise

If the Investor does not exercise the Tag-Along Right within the 15 Business Day period, the Founder may proceed with the proposed transfer on the terms specified in the Tag Notice without further obligation to the Investor.

08

General provisions

8.1
Confidentiality

Each Party shall keep confidential all information relating to the other Parties and to the Business that is not in the public domain, and shall not disclose such information to any third party without prior written consent, except as required by law or regulation.

8.2
Entire agreement

This agreement, together with the Articles and any documents referred to herein, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and understandings between them.

8.3
Amendments

No amendment to this agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by all Parties.

8.4
Governing law

This agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Scotland. The company is incorporated in Scotland under company number SC891475. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Scottish courts.

8.5
Severance

If any provision of this agreement is found to be invalid, unenforceable, or illegal, that provision shall be severed from the agreement and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

8.6
No partnership

Nothing in this agreement shall constitute or be deemed to constitute a partnership, joint venture, or agency between the Parties.

8.7
Counterparts

This agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which when executed shall constitute an original, and all counterparts together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall be valid and binding.
