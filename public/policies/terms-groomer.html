1. About these Groomer Terms

These Groomer Terms apply to professional dog groomers who list services on Groomr. They sit alongside our Platform Terms of Use, our Privacy Policy, and our Groomer Verification Policy. By creating a Groomer account or listing services, you confirm that you have read and accepted these Groomer Terms.

Groomr is operated by Unhinged Development Group Ltd, trading as Groomr (“we”, “us”, “Groomr”). You can contact us at hello@groomr.uk for general queries, partners@groomr.uk for groomer onboarding, or legal@groomr.uk for legal matters.

2. The Relationship Between You and Groomr

2.1 You are independent

You are an independent business. You are not an employee, worker, agent, partner, or joint-venturer of Groomr. Nothing in these Terms creates an employment or worker relationship between us. You are responsible for your own tax, National Insurance, VAT (if applicable), insurance, pension, and all other obligations attaching to your business.

2.2 The customer contract is yours

When an Owner books an appointment with you through the Platform, the contract for the grooming service is formed directly between you and the Owner. Groomr is not a party to that contract. We provide the technology that enables the booking, payment, and communication around it, but the legal responsibility for the service — including under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 — is yours.

2.3 What this means in practice

  • You set your own services, prices, opening hours and availability.
  • You decide whether to accept or refuse a particular booking, subject to your stated availability and the booking flow rules.
  • You provide your own premises, tools, equipment, products and consumables.
  • You hold your own insurance and qualifications (see section 4).
  • You are responsible for the quality and safety of every appointment you accept.

3. Eligibility and Verification

3.1 Who can register

To register as a Groomer you must:

  • be at least 18 years old;
  • be a UK resident and operate your grooming business in the United Kingdom;
  • either trade as a sole trader registered with HMRC, or operate through a UK-registered limited company or partnership of which you are an authorised representative;
  • hold the qualifications and insurance required by the Groomer Verification Policy; and
  • not have been previously removed from the Platform for cause.

3.2 The Verified badge

Groomr operates a Verified badge scheme, set out in full in the Groomer Verification Policy. The badge does not constitute an employment relationship, an endorsement of any particular booking, or a guarantee of outcome. It is a trust signal indicating that we have reviewed your documents. You may list and trade on the Platform without the badge, but verified Groomers benefit from search prominence and the trust signal to Owners.

3.3 Document upkeep

You are responsible for keeping your verification documents current. In particular:

  • Public liability insurance must be renewed before expiry and the renewed certificate uploaded promptly; failure to do so within 14 days of expiry will result in revocation of the Verified badge.
  • Canine first aid certification must be re-submitted every 3 years.
  • If you employ staff, employers’ liability insurance must be maintained at a minimum of £5,000,000 cover and the certificate must be current.

4. Insurance, Welfare and Legal Compliance

4.1 Mandatory insurance

You must maintain, at your own cost, throughout your time on the Platform:

  • public liability insurance of at least £1,000,000 covering professional dog grooming activities;
  • employers’ liability insurance of at least £5,000,000 if you have any employees (a UK legal requirement); and
  • any other insurance required by your local authority or by the nature of your business.

Professional indemnity, equipment and stock insurance are strongly recommended but not currently required.

4.2 Animal welfare

You agree to operate in full compliance with the Animal Welfare Act 2006 and (if you trade in Scotland) the Animal Welfare (Scotland) Act 2006, and to uphold the Five Freedoms as the minimum standard of care for every dog in your charge. Any credible report of animal mistreatment will result in immediate account suspension pending investigation, irrespective of your verification status.

4.3 Local authority licensing

Some local authorities require a licence to operate a dog grooming business. It is your responsibility to confirm whether you require any licence, registration or permit in your area, and to hold it before accepting bookings.

4.4 Other legal obligations

You confirm and agree that you will comply with all laws relevant to your business, including:

  • HMRC requirements for self-employment, corporation tax and VAT;
  • UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 in respect of personal data you hold about Owners and their dogs;
  • health and safety law in respect of your premises and any staff;
  • anti-discrimination law (Equality Act 2010); and
  • consumer protection law (Consumer Rights Act 2015, Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013, Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024).

5. Your Listing and Conduct on the Platform

5.1 Honest and accurate

Your profile, services, pricing, photographs and credentials must be accurate, current and not misleading. You must not claim qualifications you do not hold, or misrepresent your facilities, experience, or breed specialisms.

5.2 No off-platform circumvention

If a booking originated through the Platform, you must take and complete that booking through the Platform, including all related payments. You must not encourage Owners (whether explicitly or implicitly) to book or pay you outside the Platform for services that originated through it. Repeat off-platform circumvention is a material breach of these Terms and may result in termination.

This restriction does not prevent you from accepting direct repeat bookings from existing customers who originally found you outside the Platform, or from operating other booking channels in parallel for unrelated customers.

5.3 Reviews

You may not solicit fake reviews, pay for reviews, or offer Owners any inducement (including discounts) in exchange for positive reviews. The Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 makes this conduct expressly illegal and we will report serious cases to the Competition and Markets Authority. You may respond to reviews professionally; you may not threaten or harass an Owner in response to a review.

5.4 Communication with Owners

Use the Platform messaging system for communications relating to bookings. Be professional, respectful, and timely. You must not use the Platform to send marketing communications to Owners that they have not opted in to. You must not use personal data obtained through a booking for any purpose other than performing that booking and (where lawful) following up with that Owner.

6. Bookings

6.1 Availability and acceptance

You control your availability via the Platform. Slots you mark as available are open for booking. Where you have enabled instant booking, a confirmed booking is binding on you when the Owner confirms it. Where you require manual acceptance, you should respond within the timeframe set in your Groomer dashboard.

6.2 Your cancellation policy

You may select one of the deposit tiers described in the Owner Terms (No Deposit, Fixed Deposit, Full Prepayment) and an associated cancellation policy. Owners are bound to that policy on booking. Your cancellation policy must comply with consumer law — in particular, forfeiture amounts must reflect a genuine pre-estimate of your loss and not be a penalty. Groomr reserves the right to adjust cancellation policies that we consider unfair.

6.3 If you cancel

If you cancel a confirmed booking for any reason other than the Owner’s breach (for example, your own illness, equipment failure, or scheduling conflict), the Owner is entitled to a full refund of any deposit or prepayment. We monitor cancellations by Groomers; repeated short-notice cancellations affect your search ranking and may result in suspension.

6.4 Refusal of service

You may, in your professional judgment, refuse or stop a service if the dog is unsafe to handle, unwell, in pain, or if a requested service is not in the dog’s best interest. You may charge a reasonable handling fee where the refusal arises from a matter the Owner failed to declare at booking. Refusals must be respectful and not discriminatory within the meaning of the Equality Act 2010.

If an Owner raises a dispute, you will be notified through the Platform and asked to respond with your account of the matter and any supporting evidence (photographs, notes, communications). Where a dispute is upheld in the Owner’s favour, Groomr may direct Stripe to refund the Owner in whole or in part from the held funds. This direction is given as part of the Platform service and does not preclude any independent claim you may have against the Owner in respect of the underlying service.

10. Suspension and Termination

10.1 By you

You may close your Groomer account at any time by emailing partners@groomr.uk. Your profile will be hidden from search immediately. Any confirmed future bookings must either be honoured by you or cancelled with refunds to the affected Owners. Your account will be closed once all confirmed bookings are resolved.

10.2 By us

We may suspend or terminate your account immediately and without prior notice if:

  • there is a credible report of animal mistreatment or a welfare concern;
  • you breach the Animal Welfare Act 2006 or the Animal Welfare (Scotland) Act 2006;
  • you commit a fundamental breach of these Terms;
  • you provide false or fraudulent verification documents;
  • you engage in fraud, harassment, discrimination, or unlawful conduct on or through the Platform;
  • you become insolvent, enter liquidation, or are subject to a similar process;
  • you are required to be suspended by a court, regulator, or applicable law.

We may also suspend or terminate on 30 days’ notice if:

  • you persistently fail to meet service standards;
  • you fail to keep verification documents current;
  • you have a high rate of cancellations, no-shows, or upheld disputes; or
  • we decide to withdraw the Platform from your area or to discontinue the service.

10.3 Effect of termination

On termination, your listing will be removed from search and you will no longer be able to receive new bookings. Confirmed bookings must be honoured or cancelled. Funds held by Stripe will be released or refunded in accordance with the rules above. Reviews and booking history remain on the Platform. Provisions relating to indemnity, liability, intellectual property, and any other provision intended to survive termination shall continue to apply.
