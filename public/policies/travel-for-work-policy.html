1. About This Policy

This policy covers travel required for work purposes — how it is authorised, how bookings are made, acceptable standards for travel and accommodation, driving for work, and the company's duty of care to employees who travel.

This policy applies to all employees who travel for work. Commuting to a regular workplace is not covered. The costs of work travel are reimbursed through the Expenses Policy, which should be read alongside this one.

This policy does not apply to independent groomers or contractors on the Groomr platform. It is governed by Scots law. Employment claims arising under it fall within the jurisdiction of Employment Tribunals Scotland.

2. Authorisation

All work travel must be approved by the Founder or your line manager before bookings are made. Submit a brief request stating the purpose of travel, destination, proposed dates, and estimated cost. Do not book travel on the assumption approval will follow.

The company expects travel to have a clear, documented business purpose — meetings, conferences, partner visits, or investor commitments. Speculative or convenience travel is not funded.

Travel time to a destination that is not your regular workplace counts as working time under the Working Time Regulations 1998. If work travel would push your hours significantly above normal, discuss this with your manager in advance.

3. Booking Travel & Accommodation

3.1 Booking Process

Employees book their own travel and claim costs back through the expenses process. There is no central booking system. Book as far in advance as reasonably possible to keep costs down.

3.2 Class of Travel

The company's travel standard is economy class for all journeys:

  • Rail: Standard class for all journeys.
  • Flights: Economy class for all flights. No business class, regardless of journey length.
  • Preference for rail: Where rail is practicable (broadly, journeys under 3 hours), it is the expected mode. Flights for domestic journeys require Founder approval.

3.3 Accommodation

Where an overnight stay is required:

  • Book a standard hotel room at a reasonable rate. Use the cheapest option that meets basic comfort and safety standards.
  • Airbnb and short-let rentals are not permitted. Groomr's employer liability insurance does not extend to short-let properties, and some policies specifically exclude them.
  • An accommodation cap per night may be introduced as travel frequency grows. In the meantime, apply reasonable judgement and discuss any unusually high-cost city bookings with the Founder first.

4. Driving for Work

If you drive for work purposes — including using your personal vehicle — the following requirements apply:

  • You must hold a valid driving licence at all times. You must notify the Founder immediately if your licence is endorsed, suspended, or revoked.
  • If using a personal vehicle, your motor insurance must include business use cover. Standard personal policies typically exclude driving for work. Driving for work without business use cover is an offence and may invalidate any insurance claim.
  • Do not drive while fatigued. If a journey is long, break it up. The company will not require you to drive in conditions or for distances that create a safety risk.
  • Mobile phone use while driving is prohibited, including hands-free in circumstances where it impairs concentration.

Mileage driven for work in your personal vehicle is reimbursed at HMRC Approved Mileage Allowance Payment (AMAP) rates: 45p per mile for the first 10,000 miles in a tax year, 25p per mile thereafter. Claim via the expenses process with a mileage log showing start, end, and purpose of each journey.

5. Duty of Care & Emergencies

The company's duty of care under the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974 extends to employees while they are travelling for work. This includes travel in the UK and abroad.

When travelling for work, employees must:

  • Ensure the Founder or a colleague knows your itinerary, destination, and expected return time for any trip involving overnight stays or travel to unfamiliar locations.
  • Carry an emergency contact number for the Founder or a designated company contact.
  • Notify the Founder promptly if plans change significantly — for example, if a return is delayed or if you experience a safety concern.

If you have a travel-related emergency, contact the Founder directly. In a serious emergency — injury, crime, or a situation requiring immediate assistance — contact local emergency services first (999 in the UK, or the relevant local number abroad), then notify the Founder as soon as it is safe to do so.

6. International Travel

International business travel is restricted and requires Founder approval in each case. It is not a standard entitlement and will be approved only where there is a clear and documented business need.

Before travelling internationally for work, the following steps must be completed:

  • Check the FCDO travel advice for the destination country at gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice and follow any guidance issued.
  • Confirm you hold a valid passport and, where required, the appropriate visa or entry documentation.
  • Arrange travel insurance — this is reimbursable as a travel expense. Do not travel internationally for work without insurance.
  • Apply the data security requirements from the Remote Working Policy, with particular attention to border crossing risks (see below).

Devices at borders: Border authorities can require access to devices. Do not travel internationally with a company laptop containing sensitive user or company data without speaking to the Founder first. Remote wipe is available — know how to trigger it before you travel.

7. Expenses

All travel costs — transport, accommodation, meals while away, and incidental business expenses — are reimbursed through the expenses process. Full details of what can and cannot be claimed, receipt requirements, meal subsistence caps, and submission timelines are in the Expenses Policy.

Travel insurance for international trips is a claimable expense. Keep the receipt and submit it alongside your other travel costs.

Commuting to your regular place of work is not a claimable travel expense. If you are uncertain whether a particular journey qualifies, check with the Founder before incurring the cost.
