# Central Scotland Dog Groomer Outreach List

**Purpose:** Prospect list for Groomr onboarding outreach.
**Coverage:** Scotland's central belt, east coast → west coast.
**Compiled:** June 2026.

---

## How to use this document

This is a **lead list**, organised by council area and then by town/city, running
geographically from the East Lothian coast across to the Firth of Clyde. Each row
is one grooming business with whatever contact details could be sourced, plus —
where detectable — the **online booking/management software they already use**.

### Booking software as a sales signal

The "Booking software" column is the most strategically useful field for outreach
sequencing:

- **`phone/social only`** — takes bookings by phone, text/WhatsApp or Facebook DM,
  with no online booking system. **These are the warmest Groomr prospects** — they
  have an unmet need and no switching cost from an incumbent platform.
- **Named platform (Booksy, Fresha, MoeGo, Savvy Pet Spa, Setmore, Square, SumUp,
  HeyGoldie, ProPet)** — already paying for booking software. These are
  *switch/upsell* targets: harder to win, but they've demonstrated willingness to
  pay for exactly this category of product. Lead with differentiation/price.
- **`unknown`** — the business exists and is sourced, but the booking method
  couldn't be confirmed (often because the site blocked automated reads). Treat as
  "to be qualified on first contact".

### Incumbent platforms seen in the wild (central belt)

| Platform | Where it shows up | Notes for positioning |
|---|---|---|
| **Booksy** | Motherwell, Lanark, Falkirk, Paisley, southside Glasgow | Most common third-party platform among independents here |
| **Fresha** | Glasgow (Elite Dog Spa, Bespoke, The Garden), Motherwell, Greenock, Gourock, Dumbarton, Clydebank, Denny, Alva | Second most common; free-tier hook + payment fees |
| **MoeGo** | Dofos (Edinburgh chain), Temple of the Dog (Dalkeith), Oh My Dog (Dumbarton) | Grooming-specific; appears with more "premium" salons |
| **Savvy Pet Spa** | Blantyre, Bonnybridge | UK pet-specific software |
| **Setmore / Square / SumUp / HeyGoldie** | One-offs (Inverkip, Edinburgh) | Generalist tools repurposed for grooming |
| **ProPet / in-house** | Pets at Home "Groom Room" branches | National chain — low priority for outreach |

**Headline takeaway:** the large majority of independent groomers across the
central belt are still **phone/social-only**. That is the core addressable market.

### Data quality & methodology

- Gathered via web search across town-specific queries plus business-name lookups,
  corroborated against business websites, their booking pages, Facebook/Instagram,
  and directories (Yell, Thomson Local, Yelp, The Good Dog Guide, StarOfService,
  Cylex, Bark, Pata.pet, Companies House).
- **No contact details were invented.** Every phone number, address and email was
  taken from a source that stated it. Fields with no sourced value read
  `not found` / `unknown`.
- Many small-business sites and Booksy/Fresha pages return HTTP 403 to automated
  fetching, so some details come from indexed search snippets rather than direct
  page reads. **Verify load-bearing details (especially phone numbers, which can be
  stale) before a campaign.**
- Entries flagged *"verify before outreach"* / *"name not confirmed"* surfaced as
  addresses or directory mentions without a confirmed trading name — qualify these
  first.
- **Scope note:** the core is the central belt proper (regions 1–6). **Fife
  (region 7)** and **Ayrshire — North, East & South (region 8)** have been added
  as adjacent expansion clusters.
