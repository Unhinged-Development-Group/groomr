Design System & Brand Guidelines

Meet

This document defines the Groomr brand — how it looks, sounds, and behaves. It is our single source of truth for interfaces, marketing, and brand expressions.

What Groomr is.

The Brand Story

Everything starts with the dog.

Groomr is a consumer-first marketplace connecting dog owners with trusted local groomers — and giving those groomers the tools to run a brilliant business. Instant booking, verified reviews, and a powerful dashboard. Better for owners. Better for groomers.

Groomr started with a simple frustration — finding a great local groomer shouldn't be this hard. After one too many dead ends searching for someone trustworthy to look after Murphy, our long-haired chihuahua, we knew there had to be a better way.

So we built one. A place where dog owners can find, book, and trust a local groomer — and where independent groomers get the tools and visibility they deserve.

Products in this system

  • Consumer Website: Landing page, search results, groomer profiles, customer dashboard. Public, conversion-driven.
  • Groomer Dashboard: Appointment management, client list, business tools. Authenticated.
  • Note: A native mobile app is not currently planned. Our web products are fully mobile-responsive.

Mission Statement

To make dog grooming effortless for owners and rewarding for groomers — by connecting local communities through a platform built on trust, simplicity, and a genuine love of dogs.

Vision Statement

A world where every dog has a groomer they love, and every groomer has a business they're proud of.

Core Values

Trust first.

We only feature groomers we'd trust with our own dogs.

Community over competition.

We champion independent groomers, not corporate chains.

Simple by design.

Booking a groom should take seconds, not a Sunday afternoon.

Dogs at the centre.

Every decision we make starts with what's best for the dog.

Do's, Don'ts & Imagery

The "Do's"

  • • Do let the Cream breathe: Use Alabaster Cream generously as your primary background. Empty space is a designer's best friend.
  • • Do rely on Deep Slate: Heavy lifting for headings, body text, primary icons. High contrast without the harshness of pure black.
  • • Do use Gold to drive action: Reserve it for your most critical interaction points. Used sparingly, it immediately draws the eye.
  • • Do use real imagery: Warm, naturally lit, candid photography of dogs and groomers. Crop tight on faces, fur texture, and expression.

The "Don't" Checklist

  • • Don't use Groomr Gold for typography.
  • • Don't layer Muted Terracotta directly on Sage Leaf — it vibrates and strains the eyes.
  • • Don't make everything colourful. Let Alabaster Cream do the heavy lifting.
  • • Don't use gradients, textures, or patterns. No hand-drawn illustrations or glassmorphism overlays.
  • • No AI-generated dogs. Ever. Real dogs, real people.

Minimum Size

Digital: no smaller than 120px wide for the full lockup, 32px for the standalone mark. Print: no smaller than 30mm wide.

Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the "g" in the wordmark. Nothing should enter this zone.

Improper Usage

Never stretch, rotate, randomly recolour outside official colorways, place over busy backgrounds, or add effects like shadows.

Text vs. Logo

When referring to the brand in body copy, headlines, or general text, always type it out as Groomr (capital G). Never use the logo graphic inline as a replacement for the written word.

Typography

Primary Typeface

Fredoka Bold

Reserved for major moments — H1 and H2. Hero headers, campaign headlines, and big brand expressions. Soft, rounded, warm.

Secondary Typeface

Nunito

The workhorse (H3, body, UI). Use for all body text, subheadings, UI labels, and anything that needs to be read rather than felt.

Technical & Casing Rules

  • Webfonts: Both ship as local variable TTFs (`fonts/Fredoka-VariableFont...`). No Google Fonts dependency in production.
  • Brand Name: Always written as Groomr — capital G, no full stop.
  • Headings (H1 & H2): Title Case — capitalise every major word.
  • Body text & H3: Sentence case — first word and proper nouns only.
  • Eyebrows / Meta labels: UPPERCASE with `0.10em` or `0.15em` letter-spacing.
  • Buttons: Title Case for a confident feel (e.g., "Sign Up Now").

Voice & Messaging

The Groomr voice is warm, direct, and quietly confident. We're the tool that connects dog owners with local experts who care as much as they do.

1. Warm & Approachable

We speak to users like a knowledgeable local friend, not a faceless tech company.

2. Direct & Simple

Booking should be effortless. We use short sentences, clear actions, and zero jargon.

3. Quietly Confident

We don't need to shout. We let the quality of our platform and our trusted groomers do the talking.

4. Dog-Obsessed

We genuinely care. Every message we write ultimately comes back to the dog's wellbeing and happiness.

Grammar & Phrasing Rules

  • Voice (You / We): Speak in first-person plural ("we built this", "we care") for brand-led copy. Address users in second-person ("your dog", "you'll get") for product copy. Never say "the user" or "consumers".
  • Punctuation: British English spellings throughout (colour, centre, realise). Em dashes (—) are used minimally in marketing copy. Contractions are encouraged (don't, we're).
  • Brand Name: Always written as Groomr (capital G, no full stop). Never use the logo graphic inline as a replacement.
  • No Emoji: Emoji are not part of the brand. They feel inconsistent with the considered voice.
  • Puns: One pun is plenty. Avoid "fur baby" or "pawsome". Do not undermine credibility.

Write This

"Find a local groomer in minutes. Real availability, instant confirmation — no phone tag."

Direct, specific, solves a real problem. Mentions the pain point without dwelling on it.

Not This

"Welcome to Groomr, your premier destination for professional canine grooming services..."

Corporate, vague, zero personality. Reads like a terms page.

Write This

"Your dog deserves a regular. Someone who knows their name, their coat, and exactly how they like their ears done."

Emotionally true, specific, earns the tagline rather than just stating it.

Not This

"Our amazing groomers are pawsome professionals who will make your fur baby look their absolute best!"

Trying too hard. "Fur baby" undermines credibility. One pun is plenty.

Write This

"Oops! Something went wrong. Let's try that again."

Human, not panicked. Acknowledges the error without drama.

Not This

"An unexpected error has occurred. Please contact support."

Cold, corporate. Passive voice. Puts the burden on the user.

The Tagline

"Your dog deserves a regular."

Always Quoted. Always Italics. Always Full Stop. Never Paraphrased.

A "regular" isn't just a booking; it's a relationship. It's the comfort of a groomer who knows your dog by name, understands their unique coat, and always has their favourite treat waiting. Every time we use these words, we are promising that personal bond.

Iconography Library

Groomr uses a custom inline-SVG icon system. It is the brand's most distinctive UI signature. Below is the complete collection of 30 standard icons built with our consistent circular splash logic.

Construction Guidelines

Each icon is composed of two layers: an offset coloured "splash" circle behind the glyph, and a Deep Slate stroke glyph on top.

  • • Stroke Rules: STRICTLY use a stroke-width="1.5" with stroke-linecap="round" & stroke-linejoin="round" in Deep Slate.
  • • The Splash Shape: Always use a perfect circle. No exceptions.
  • • Size & Offset: The circle's radius and offset vary organically to add character, but must not exceed the viewBox.
  • • Splash Colors: Alternate between Groomr Gold (#eae45c) and Muted Terracotta (#c87964) at opacity="0.55".

UI Application & Components

Spacing, Radii & Shadows

Technical Specifications Use Alabaster Cream to let elements breathe. Spacing should follow a strict 4px/8px baseline grid. Border radii are scaled semantically: 12px for standard cards, full pills for primary buttons. Shadows are subtle and use Pebble Grey (#95a5a6) with low opacity to avoid muddying the interface.

Technical Specifications Fully rounded (pill-shaped) borders. Transition backgrounds smoothly over 0.3 seconds. Active/Focus states should feature a crisp 3px solid outline in Groomr Gold. Primary buttons transition from Groomr Gold to Muted Terracotta on hover.

Technical Specifications Pills are purely informational and not interactive. Use a 10% or 20% opacity background of the semantic color, matched with a slightly darker (30% to 40% opacity) border and fully solid text.
