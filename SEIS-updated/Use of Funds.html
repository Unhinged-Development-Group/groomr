Use of Investment Funds

01

Purpose of this document

This document sets out how the £10,000 raised through the SEIS share issue will be deployed across Groomr's qualifying trade activities. It is prepared in accordance with HMRC's requirement that companies seeking SEIS Advance Assurance provide details of all trading activities to be carried out and how much is expected to be spent on each activity.

Groomr confirms that the entirety of the investment will be spent within three years of the date of share issue on activities directly related to the growth and development of its qualifying trade — the operation and scaling of a two-sided dog grooming marketplace in the United Kingdom.

A note on risk

The £10,000 investment is being deployed into a pre-revenue, founder-led business with no guaranteed commercial outcome. The costs below represent what is required to launch and attempt to scale the platform — not a guarantee of success. Several cost lines, particularly marketing and payment processing, scale with activity and will increase materially as the platform grows. The investment may be fully consumed before the business reaches self-sustainability.

SEIS deployment commitment

The full £10,000 raised by this share issue will be spent within three years of the date of issue on the qualifying trading activities of Groomr Ltd, as detailed in this document. No part of the investment will be held as a long-term cash reserve, used to repay existing liabilities, or deployed outside the company's qualifying trade.

03

The Groomer Sign-Up Incentive

Every groomer joining the platform has their first 150 bookings processed commission-free. Groomr absorbs the Stripe and Plivo costs on those bookings — a fixed acquisition cost of approximately £210 per groomer. This is an always-on offer with no time restriction.

At 20 bookings per week, a full-time groomer completes their 150-booking incentive window in approximately seven weeks, at which point the standard 8% commission applies to all subsequent bookings.

Year 1 targets 150 active groomers. At £210 per groomer, the total incentive cost across the full Year 1 cohort is approximately £31,500. This is funded by commission revenue from groomers who have already completed their incentive window — not from the seed investment. The investment covers only the first cohort of groomers before commission revenue begins.

Where the investment is genuinely at risk

The 150-groomer Year 1 target is ambitious for a newly launched platform with no prior trading history. If groomer onboarding is slower than projected, the incentive cost per month will be lower — but commission revenue will also take longer to materialise. The platform must successfully recruit, onboard, and retain a critical mass of groomers in its initial markets for the model to become self-sustaining. There is no guarantee this will occur within the timeframes projected.

If growth is slower than projected

If groomer onboarding takes longer than anticipated, the platform will remain cash-negative for longer and the seed investment will be consumed more quickly relative to the revenue it generates. At the fixed cost base of £448/month, and with slower groomer adoption, the £10,000 investment could be fully consumed before the platform reaches self-sustainability. This is a genuine risk and is reflected in the Risk to Capital statement accompanying this document.

06

Three-year deployment plan

All investment funds will be deployed within three years of the date of share issue on qualifying trading activities, in line with SEIS requirements.

Year 1
Launch & initial traction

Seed investment fully deployed against infrastructure, setup, marketing, travel, and first groomer cohort onboarding. Target 150 active groomers by year end. Platform expected to reach cash-positive by Month 3–4.

Year 2
Expansion & diversification

Platform self-funded from commission. Geographic expansion into additional cities. Groomer subscription tier introduced. Affiliate revenue from pet insurance partners begins. Marketing spend increases with coverage.

Year 3
National scale

Target 900 active groomers. Revenue diversified across commission, subscriptions, and affiliate income. All seed investment fully deployed well within the three-year SEIS qualifying window.

Confirmation of deployment within 3 years

Groomr Ltd confirms that the full £10,000 raised by this share issue will be deployed within three years of the date of issue on the qualifying trading activities described in this document. Based on the cost structure above, the investment is expected to be fully deployed within the first 12 months of trading.
