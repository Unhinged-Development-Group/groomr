1. About This Policy

This policy governs how employees of Unhinged Development Group Ltd (trading as Groomr) work remotely. It covers the contractual basis for remote working, availability expectations, workspace requirements, data security, the home-working allowance, and working abroad.

This policy applies to all employees. It does not apply to independent groomers or contractors on the Groomr platform.

This policy is distinct from the Flexible Working Policy, which governs the process for formally changing the terms of your working arrangement. This policy governs how remote working operates once an arrangement is in place.

Processing of personal data in a remote working context is covered by the Employee Privacy Notice. This policy is governed by Scots law. Employment claims arising under it fall within the jurisdiction of Employment Tribunals Scotland.

2. Our Default Arrangement

Remote working is the contractual default at Groomr. Unless your employment contract specifies otherwise, you are expected to work from home as your primary place of work.

Because remote working is contractual — not discretionary — it cannot be unilaterally withdrawn by the company without agreement or a contractual variation process. If operational circumstances were ever to require a change to this arrangement, the company would consult with you and follow a fair process.

Working from a location other than your home address (for example, a café or co-working space) is permitted, provided you can work securely and meet the data security requirements set out in section 5. You remain responsible for the security of company data and devices wherever you work.

3. Core Hours & Availability

Core hours and availability expectations are role-dependent and will be set out in your employment contract or confirmed with you by your line manager at the start of your employment.

As a general principle, you are expected to be reachable during the hours your role requires. For most roles this means being available and responsive during the standard working day. Where your role involves collaboration with colleagues or external parties, align your availability with those needs.

You are not expected to be available outside your contracted hours. The company does not operate an always-on culture. If you work outside your contracted hours regularly, raise this with your line manager — it may indicate a workload problem that should be addressed, not normalised.

4. DSE Assessment

The Display Screen Equipment Regulations 1992 require the company to assess the workstation of any employee who habitually uses a screen at home. As a remote-first company, this applies to all employees.

You will be asked to complete a DSE self-assessment within your first two weeks of employment. The self-assessment form will be provided to you on joining.

Your home workspace should meet these minimum standards:

  • A dedicated desk or table with sufficient space for your screen, keyboard, and mouse.
  • A chair that supports your lower back, with your feet flat on the floor or on a footrest.
  • Adequate natural or artificial lighting without glare on the screen.
  • A screen position at approximately arm's length with the top of the screen at or slightly below eye level.

Where the self-assessment identifies a problem — for example, an unsuitable chair — please flag this and the company will consider providing or contributing to the cost of appropriate equipment. If you require spectacles specifically for screen work, the company will contribute to the cost of an eye test; speak to the Founder.

5. Security & Equipment

5.1 Company Equipment

The company provides a laptop and any necessary peripherals. You must use company-provided equipment for all work activity. Full terms governing acceptable use, security requirements, and return obligations are in the Use of Company Equipment Policy.

5.2 Data Security at Home

The following security requirements apply at all times when working remotely:

  • Your home Wi-Fi must be password-protected. Do not use public Wi-Fi for any work activity without connecting via a VPN first.
  • Your screen must be locked when you step away from your desk. Set your device to lock automatically after no more than 10 minutes idle.
  • Do not save work files to personal cloud storage (personal iCloud, Google Drive, Dropbox, or equivalent). Use only company-approved storage.
  • Multi-factor authentication must be enabled on all work accounts. This is non-negotiable — Groomr's platform holds user payment and personal data.
  • Position your screen so that it cannot be viewed by others in shared spaces.
  • Print as little as possible. Any printed work documents containing personal data must be securely destroyed (shredded) rather than placed in general recycling.

5.3 Monitoring

The company uses Google Workspace tools, which provide usage logs and administrative visibility over company accounts and devices. This is disclosed here and in the Employee Privacy Notice. No covert monitoring is in place. If monitoring practice changes, employees will be informed and the Privacy Notice updated.

6. Home-Working Allowance

The company pays employees the HMRC-approved home-working allowance of £6 per week (tax-free) to contribute towards the additional costs of working from home — heating, lighting, and broadband.

The allowance is paid through payroll alongside your regular salary. No receipts are required — it is a flat-rate contribution in line with HMRC's approved amount for employer-paid allowances.

If you believe your actual additional costs exceed this allowance significantly, you may be able to claim tax relief directly from HMRC for the difference. This is a matter between you and HMRC and does not require employer involvement.

7. Working Abroad

Working from outside the UK is restricted and requires prior approval from the Founder in each case. It is not a default entitlement.

Even short periods of working abroad can trigger complex tax, employment law, and social security obligations in the host country — regardless of whether you remain a UK employee and regardless of how short the period is. The company will seek appropriate advice before approving any overseas working arrangement.

If you wish to work abroad temporarily — for example, while travelling — submit a request to the Founder in advance, stating the country, the proposed dates, and the nature of your work during that period. Do not make travel arrangements on the assumption that approval will be granted.

Important: At border crossings, authorities may request access to devices. If you travel with a company laptop, notify the Founder in advance. Do not unlock or hand over a device containing company or user data without first contacting the Founder. Device remote-wipe capability is in place for this scenario.

8. Reviewing Arrangements

Because remote working is a contractual term of employment at Groomr, it will not be changed without following a fair process. This includes giving reasonable notice, consulting with you, and — if a permanent change were ever proposed — following the correct contractual variation procedure.

If you want to change your working arrangement — for example, to move to hybrid working or to change your hours — you have the right to make a formal flexible working request from day one of employment. The process for doing so is set out in the Flexible Working Policy.

If you have a concern about how remote working is affecting your health, workload, or ability to do your job, please raise it with your line manager or the Founder. The earlier a concern is raised, the easier it is to address.
