The Problem We're Solving

The problem at the heart of Groomr is not a niche frustration — it is structural to how the UK's independent grooming sector operates today.

The Pet Owner Experience

This is an entirely unnecessary barrier. Consumers already book taxis, hair appointments, and restaurant tables via clean, instant platforms.

When a dog owner moves to a new area — or simply needs a new groomer — the process looks remarkably like it did fifteen years ago:

  • • A Google search returns a handful of results, most leading to outdated or non-existent websites.
  • • The groomer's most recent activity is on Facebook or Instagram, requiring the owner to have an active social media account.
  • • Booking is done via direct message — introducing delays, ambiguity, and unnecessary back-and-forth.
  • • Pricing, availability, and groomer credentials are rarely surfaced upfront — everything requires a conversation.
  • • Confirmation is informal, often just a message thread, with no structured reminder or cancellation policy.

This is an entirely unnecessary barrier. Consumers already book taxis, hair appointments, and restaurant tables via clean, instant platforms. There is no logical reason grooming should be any different.

The Groomer Experience

Independent groomers are skilled professionals running small businesses, often solo, often from home. But the tools they rely on are not designed for them:

  • • Social media DMs mix personal and business communication, creating chaos and missed enquiries.
  • • There is no single view of bookings, clients, or revenue — it lives across notebooks, spreadsheets, and message threads.
  • • No-shows are common with no automated reminder or deposit mechanism in place.
  • • Word-of-mouth is the dominant acquisition channel, limiting growth and creating feast-or-famine booking cycles.
  • • Groomers who want a more professional presence must build their own website — a skill most lack and a cost many cannot justify.

Supply-Side Viability

The Administrative Burden

To succeed, a marketplace must solve problems for both the consumer and the supplier. While pet owners want seamless booking, independent groomers are desperate for comprehensive business management tools. Industry forums are filled with complaints regarding the highly fragmented method of managing a business across Instagram DMs, WhatsApp, SMS, and phone calls.

  • •
    Time Wasted: Independent groomers typically waste up to 24% of their working week on administrative tasks[8]. This translates to roughly 1 to 2 hours a day lost to manual scheduling and chasing payments — time that could be spent grooming an additional dog (equivalent to £45-£60 lost revenue daily).
  • •
    Burnout: The constant ping of DMs from clients arguing over slots, failing to read policies, or cancelling last minute leads to severe groomer burnout. They want to be with dogs, not acting as full-time receptionists.

The B2B SaaS Opportunity

There is immense, validated demand from groomers for an all-in-one, modern web platform. B2B software like MoeGo, Gingr, and Groomsoft prove that groomers will actively use (and pay for) comprehensive back-end tools.

By offering a web-based ecosystem that includes fully automated booking, integrated financials, centralised messaging, detailed pet profiles, and review management, we are not just providing a "marketplace listing."

We are providing a Business-in-a-Box.

If you can provide these essential B2B tools while simultaneously driving net-new clients to them via the consumer marketplace, the value proposition to the groomer becomes undeniable.
