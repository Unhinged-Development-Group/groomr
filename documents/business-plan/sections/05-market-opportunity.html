Market Opportunity

Market Size

The UK pet grooming services market was valued at approximately USD 562.9 million in 2024 and is projected to reach USD 804.8 million by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 6.4%.[6] Dogs account for over 87% of this revenue.

An estimated 12 million dogs live in the UK. Some 30% of dog owners visit a grooming parlour in any given year, while 84% arrange at least one professional groom annually.[7] With approximately 10,600 groomers serving this demand — the majority independent, the majority without formal online booking — the infrastructure gap is obvious.

Structural Tailwinds

  • • Pet humanisation: UK pet owners increasingly treat animals as family members, spending more on premium professional care.
  • • Post-pandemic growth: Pet ownership surged during COVID-19 and has not reversed, creating a persistently larger customer base.
  • • Digital expectations: Consumers under 45 expect to book services online and are increasingly reluctant to engage via social media for commercial transactions.
  • • Independent groomer fragmentation: The vast majority of UK groomers are sole traders without a digital booking presence — ripe for aggregation.

Competitive Landscape

The market is currently split between software built exclusively for groomers (with no consumer discovery) and broad directories (with poor grooming-specific features). Groomr sits at the perfect intersection.

Deep Dive: Tuft App

Tuft is the primary direct competitor in the UK, offering both consumer and groomer apps.

  • •
    Scale: Founded in 2021, raised ~£1.5M - £2M, reported £3.5m GMV in 2024 with over 83,000 active users.[9]
  • •
    Pain Points & Brand Weaknesses:
    • App-Only Friction: Tuft uses separated native apps instead of web, creating massive top-of-funnel drop-off for discovering local groomers.
    • The "Request" Hurdle: Despite marketing "instant booking," many appointments still require manual groomer approval.
    • Consumer Fee Backlash: Pet owners are noticeably frustrated by the £1.25 "Platform Fee" added to their checkout just for booking a service.
    • Prohibitive B2B Pricing: Their £32.99/month (or £46+ for multi-staff) subscription is difficult to justify for brand-new or small-scale independent groomers compared to a commission-only model.
    • Corporate Branding Focus: Their brand identity feels very "commercial tech startup" rather than a warm, localised, community-driven pet service.

Strategic Opportunity

Their rapid £3.5m GMV growth proves immense consumer demand exists in spite of these frustrating UI/UX barriers and fees. A frictionless, web-based platform with community-centric branding and a fairer pricing model could rapidly acquire their dissatisfied users.

Indirect Competitors (SaaS)

GroomerBooker, Reservio, MoeGo, SmartBooker: These provide phenomenal backend scheduling software for groomers.

Weakness: They are not consumer discovery marketplaces. A consumer cannot go to "MoeGo.com" to search for local groomers. Groomers must still do all their own marketing to get clients to use their personal booking links.

Tangential Competitors

Rover / Wag! / PetBacker: Massive marketplaces, but primarily focused on dog walking and sitting.

Weakness: While some groomers might list there, grooming is an afterthought on these platforms, lacking the specialised tools required to properly service the industry.
