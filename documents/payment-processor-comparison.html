The catch: GoCardless is Direct Debit, not card payments. The owner sets up a mandate on their first booking — like a gym membership or utility bill — and all future bookings pull automatically. This is actually ideal for Groomr: dog owners book recurring appointments, and Direct Debit removes the friction of re-entering card details each time.

What you lose: Customers without a UK bank account can't use it (rare for dog owners). Walk-in or first-time one-off bookings have more friction on the first booking. You'd likely want a Stripe fallback for the first transaction, then prompt the owner to set up a GoCardless mandate for future bookings.

Recommended Strategy

Short term (launch → Month 4): Start with Stripe. It's the fastest path to shipping payments, has the best developer tooling, and Connect handles marketplace payouts out of the box. The extra cost is worth not slowing down the build.

Medium term (Month 4–6): Add GoCardless as the primary payment method for returning dog owners. On the second booking, prompt owners to set up a Direct Debit mandate. This converts your recurring base (which will be most of your volume) to the much cheaper rail.

Long term (Month 6+): Once you're processing £150k+/month on cards, contact Adyen. Interchange++ pricing at that volume would make Adyen by far the cheapest card option — but only then does it make commercial sense to integrate.

Avoid: Mangopay (more expensive than Stripe for no clear benefit). Open Banking as primary (too much consumer trust friction in the UK right now).
