1. About This Policy

This policy sets out how Unhinged Development Group Ltd (trading as Groomr) supports employees in developing their skills, knowledge, and career. It applies to all employees of the company. It does not apply to independent groomers or other contractors on the Groomr platform.

Personal development and performance management are complementary — development is the positive counterpart to managing underperformance. This policy should be read alongside the Performance Management Policy.

This policy is governed by Scots law. Employment claims arising under it fall within the jurisdiction of Employment Tribunals Scotland.

2. Who Can Apply

All employees may apply for development support. There is no minimum service requirement: you can submit a request from your first week if a relevant opportunity arises.

Development opportunities must be offered and considered fairly. If the company operates a discretionary budget, decisions about who receives funding will be documented and made on objective, role-relevant grounds. We take our obligations under the Equality Act 2010 seriously — development cannot be allocated in a way that indirectly disadvantages employees with protected characteristics.

3. Development Budget

The company provides a personal development budget of £250 per employee per year. This is a guide figure — requests beyond this amount will be considered case by case where there is a clear business benefit.

The budget is not a contractual entitlement and does not carry over between years. It may be adjusted as the company grows.

Note: There is no clawback provision. If the company funds your development and you later leave, you will not be required to repay the cost.

4. What We Will Fund

The following types of development activity are within scope of this policy:

  • Courses and training programmes — online, in-person, or self-paced — directly relevant to your role or career growth.
  • Industry conferences and professional events where attendance has a clear learning or networking benefit.
  • Professional memberships and subscriptions relevant to your discipline.
  • Books, technical references, and learning resources.
  • Mentoring arrangements with an external practitioner.

Activities that are primarily personal in nature, unrelated to your current or prospective role, or that duplicate training already provided by the company are outside scope.

5. The Approval Process

Submit a development request to the Founder or a Director before committing to any cost. Requests should include a brief description of the activity, the expected benefit, and the cost.

Approval authority sits with the Founder or a Director until the company has senior managers in place, at which point approval may be delegated for requests within the standard per-head budget. Requests above the per-head budget always require Founder or Director sign-off.

Where the development involves conference attendance or off-site time during working hours, agree the timing and any operational impact with your line manager as part of the approval conversation.

The company will reimburse approved costs on submission of a valid expense claim with receipts. Development costs are reimbursed through the normal expenses process.

6. Study Leave

The company does not currently provide paid study leave — that is, paid time off to sit external exams, attend lectures, or complete coursework during working hours.

Where a course or programme requires occasional attendance during working hours (for example, a half-day workshop), this can be discussed with your line manager on a case-by-case basis. Agreement will depend on operational requirements and the relevance of the activity to your role.

Online and self-paced learning completed outside working hours is at your discretion. The company funds the cost; your time outside of working hours is your own contribution.

7. What We Ask in Return

Where the company funds your development, we ask that you apply what you have learned in your role and share relevant insights with colleagues where appropriate. This is proportionate — it is a carrot, not a condition.

There is no contractual obligation to share knowledge or to remain in employment for any period following a funded activity. However, engaging with your development in good faith is part of being a member of the team.

8. Links to Performance Reviews

Development plans should feed into your annual or half-year performance review. During your review, you and your manager will identify skills or areas you want to grow, and any development spend for the coming year can be planned in that context.

Where a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) is in place, the development support available under this policy may be used as part of the support offered. In that context, development is positioned as assistance, not as a sanction.

This policy should be read alongside the Performance Management Policy and the Employee Benefits Policy.
