1. About This Policy

This policy sets out how employees of Unhinged Development Group Ltd (trading as Groomr) claim reimbursement for expenditure incurred on company business — what is claimable, what evidence is required, and how to submit claims.

Reimbursed expenses are only tax and National Insurance-free where they are "wholly, exclusively and necessarily" incurred in the performance of employment duties, in line with HMRC rules. Claims outside these criteria may result in a taxable benefit-in-kind requiring P11D reporting. This policy is designed to keep expense claims within HMRC's qualifying categories.

This policy applies to all employees. It is governed by Scots law. Employment claims arising under it fall within the jurisdiction of Employment Tribunals Scotland.

Important: Submitting a false or inflated expense claim is gross misconduct and may result in summary dismissal and referral to the relevant authorities. If you are unsure whether something is claimable, ask before you spend.

2. What Can Be Claimed

The following categories of expenditure are claimable where they are genuinely incurred for business purposes:

  • Travel: Rail (standard class), bus, underground, taxis where justified, flights (economy only, with Founder approval). See the Travel for Work Policy for travel standards.
  • Mileage: Personal vehicle use for work journeys at HMRC AMAP rates. See section 5.
  • Accommodation: Standard hotel rooms for approved overnight stays. No Airbnb or short-let properties.
  • Meals while away: Subsistence when working away from your usual location, within the caps in section 4.
  • Business entertainment: Client or partner meals with a documented business purpose. See section 6 for HMRC rules.
  • Work-related subscriptions and tools: Software, services, or publications required for your role, approved in advance.
  • Travel insurance: For approved international business travel.
  • Personal development costs: Where approved under the Personal Development Policy.

3. What Cannot Be Claimed

The following are explicitly not claimable:

  • Commuting: Travel between your home and your regular place of work is never a claimable expense under HMRC rules.
  • Personal entertainment: Cinema, sports events, personal meals, or any expenditure of a personal nature.
  • Fines and penalties: Parking fines, congestion charges incurred through negligence, speeding fines. These are personal liabilities.
  • Alcohol above subsistence: A drink with a meal is reasonable; a bar tab is not.
  • Gifts: Gifts for clients or individuals are generally not tax-deductible and are not claimable without specific Founder approval and HMRC justification.
  • Unapproved equipment: Personal equipment purchases that have not been pre-approved.
  • Airbnb and short-let accommodation: Not permitted under the Travel for Work Policy.

4. Meal Subsistence

Meals are claimable when you are working away from your usual location and the absence means you genuinely incur additional meal costs. Meals at or near your home address are not claimable.

Breakfast: Up to £15

Lunch: Up to £15

Dinner: Up to £30

These caps are per meal, inclusive of any service charge. Alcohol is not included in the subsistence calculation — keep alcoholic drinks modest and proportionate to the meal.

Subsistence rates apply to meals you purchase for yourself. Meals for clients or business guests are subject to the business entertainment rules in section 6 rather than these subsistence caps.

Where a meal forms part of a conference or event package you have been sent to attend, do not claim subsistence for the same meal separately.

5. Mileage

Where you use your personal vehicle for an approved work journey, you may claim mileage at HMRC Approved Mileage Allowance Payment (AMAP) rates. These rates are tax-free up to the AMAP threshold — no P11D is required.

First 10,000 business miles in a tax year: 45p per mile

Over 10,000 business miles in a tax year: 25p per mile

Motorcycles: 24p per mile (all miles)

Bicycles: 20p per mile (all miles)

Submit mileage claims with a mileage log recording the date, start and end location, purpose of the journey, and number of miles. Round-trip mileage must be recorded for return journeys.

Mileage rates are set by HMRC and may change. The rates above were correct at the time this policy was published — verify current rates at gov.uk/expenses-and-benefits-business-travel-mileage if in doubt.

6. Business Entertainment

Business entertainment — meals or events for clients, partners, or investors — is claimable where there is a clear and documented business purpose.

HMRC rule: Entertaining clients or customers is not tax-deductible for the company — it cannot be offset against Corporation Tax. This is separate from the question of reimbursement. The company will reimburse justified business entertainment, but it should be understood that this expenditure comes at full cost to the business.

For every business entertainment claim, you must record at the time:

  • The names and organisations of all attendees.
  • The business purpose of the meeting.
  • The total cost and number of people.

Entertainment of employees only (for example, a team lunch) is allowable as a business expense up to the HMRC annual staff events exemption of £150 per head per year. This is a cumulative limit across all staff events in the tax year — coordinate with the Founder before booking team events to avoid inadvertently exceeding it.

7. Receipts & Submission

7.1 Receipts

A receipt is required for all claims above £10. The receipt must show the supplier name, date, amount, and — for VAT purposes — ideally the supplier's VAT number where the company is VAT-registered. A card transaction record alone is not a valid receipt.

Photographs of paper receipts are accepted. Store receipts securely until your claim is processed and confirmed.

7.2 Submission

Submit expense claims monthly, within 30 days of incurring the cost. Claims submitted more than 3 months after the expense date may not be reimbursed.

8. Company Cards

Company payment cards may be issued to employees who regularly incur business expenditure as part of their role. The decision to issue a card is made by the Founder on a justified-role basis.

If you are issued a company card:

  • Use it only for legitimate business expenses within the categories in section 2.
  • Keep receipts for every transaction and submit them through the normal expense process for reconciliation.
  • If you inadvertently use the card for a personal purchase, notify the Founder immediately and arrange to reimburse the amount promptly.
  • Return the card immediately on leaving the company or on request.

There is no cash advance facility. If you anticipate a significant outlay — for example, a high-cost conference — discuss pre-arrangement with the Founder rather than using personal funds and claiming later.
