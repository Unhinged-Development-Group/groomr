1. Who This Applies To

This Code of Conduct applies to all registered Groomr users: dog owners, independent groomers, and groomer team members. It governs how you are expected to behave on and through the Groomr platform — including in messages, listings, bookings, reviews, and all other platform interactions.

1.1 Groomer team members

Groomer team members interact with owners and dogs on behalf of a groomer account. Any conduct by a team member is attributed to the salon-owner account holder. The account holder is responsible for ensuring their team members understand and comply with this Code.

1.2 Duration

This Code applies from the point of account creation. Where conduct occurred while an account was active, this Code continues to apply to that conduct even after the account is closed.

1.3 Relationship to the Terms of Service

This Code of Conduct is incorporated into Groomr's Terms of Service. A breach of this Code is a breach of the Terms of Service and may result in enforcement action as described in Section 10.

Scope note: This Code covers conduct that occurs via Groomr's infrastructure — messaging, reviews, listings, and bookings. It does not directly govern what happens inside a grooming salon, though obligations relating to animal welfare and incident disclosure do arise during the service itself. Professional standards during the groom are addressed in the Grooming Standards Policy.

2. Conduct in Messaging

Groomr's messaging system exists to help owners and groomers discuss bookings, care requirements, and service arrangements. All users must use it for that purpose only.

2.1 Prohibited messaging conduct

The following are prohibited in all messages sent through the Groomr platform:

  • Harassment, threats, abusive language, or any communication intended to intimidate or distress another user.
  • Unsolicited promotional content or advertising for services outside of Groomr.
  • Attempting to move a booking off the Groomr platform — including sharing bank details, payment links, or alternative booking channels before or during an active booking.
  • Sending false, misleading, or deceptive communications to another user.

2.2 Obligation to communicate

If a groomer is unable to fulfil a confirmed booking, they must communicate this promptly through the Groomr platform and follow the cancellation process set out in the Terms of Service. Going silent on a confirmed booking is a breach of this Code.

3. Profile and Listing Accuracy

This section applies to groomers. All information in a groomer profile must be accurate at the time of publication and kept up to date as circumstances change.

3.1 Qualifications and credentials

Groomers must not misrepresent their qualifications or professional credentials. This includes:

  • Claiming qualifications not held — such as City & Guilds, iPET Network, or other industry accreditations.
  • Using before/after photos of another groomer's work as your own.
  • Misrepresenting years of experience or the services your salon is equipped to provide.

Groomr verifies credentials as part of the groomer onboarding process. If your circumstances change after verification, you must notify Groomr promptly.

3.2 Insurance

Groomers must hold valid professional liability insurance at the time of every booking. If your insurance lapses, you must immediately stop accepting new bookings, update your listing, and notify Groomr. Continuing to accept bookings without valid insurance is a serious breach of this Code.

3.3 Pricing and services

All services listed on your profile must accurately reflect what you offer and at the prices stated. Charging an owner a price materially different from your listed price without prior agreement may constitute a misleading commercial practice under the Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008 and is a breach of this Code.

4. Review Integrity

Reviews are the foundation of trust on Groomr. All reviews must reflect the reviewer's genuine, first-hand experience of the specific service received.

4.1 Prohibited conduct — owners

Owners must not:

  • Submit a review for a service they did not personally receive.
  • Leave a review in exchange for a payment, discount, or other incentive.
  • Submit a review on behalf of another person.
  • Participate in coordinated campaigns to post multiple negative reviews about a groomer (review-bombing).

4.2 Prohibited conduct — groomers

Groomers must not:

  • Solicit or arrange fake positive reviews.
  • Offer discounts, gifts, or other incentives conditional on receiving a positive review.
  • Pressure, threaten, or harass an owner in an attempt to prevent or remove a legitimate negative review.
  • Post false, misleading, or disproportionately retaliatory public replies to reviews.

4.3 Groomr's obligations on reviews

Groomr will not manipulate review rankings, suppress legitimate reviews, or artificially inflate ratings for any commercial reason. Any decision to remove a review — for example because it violates this Code — will be documented and communicated to the parties involved. See also Section 12.

5. Animal Welfare and Disclosure Obligations

Animal welfare obligations apply to groomers by statute under the Animal Welfare Act 2006 (England and Wales), the Animal Health & Welfare (Scotland) Act 2006, and the Welfare of Animals Act (Northern Ireland) 2011. The applicable Act follows the location of the groom. This section does not replace those statutory obligations — it describes how they interact with your use of the Groomr platform.

5.1 Groomer obligations during the groom

Groomers must:

  • Pause or stop a groom if the animal shows signs of extreme distress, acute illness, or injury, and contact the owner promptly through the Groomr platform.
  • Disclose any incident during the groom — including injury, adverse reaction, or significant distress — to the owner promptly and through the platform, so that a record is created.
  • Proactively notify Groomr of any welfare incident in addition to notifying the owner. Contact safety@groomr.uk following any incident involving injury or significant distress during a booking. Early notification supports you if a dispute arises and helps us maintain a complete and accurate record.
  • Prioritise the animal's welfare over completing the paid service where the two come into conflict.

5.2 Welfare-led refusals

A groomer may decline or stop a booking on animal-welfare grounds without this constituting a breach of this Code or the Terms of Service. Welfare-led refusals will not be counted negatively in any acceptance-rate metric if such metrics are introduced. Groomers may also require that a dog be muzzled as a safety measure — this is a reasonable professional judgement and does not constitute discriminatory conduct.

5.3 Owner disclosure obligations

Owners must accurately disclose all known relevant information at the time of booking. This includes:

  • Known aggression or reactivity toward people, other animals, or specific handling.
  • Health conditions that may affect the groom, including epilepsy, heart conditions, arthritis, or allergies.
  • Any prior incidents during grooming, including biting, fainting, or extreme distress.
  • Vaccination status where required by the groomer.

Important: Deliberately withholding known health or behavioural information in order to secure a booking is a breach of this Code. Where an owner's failure to disclose results in injury to the groomer, another animal, or the dog itself, this will be treated as a serious conduct matter.

6. Non-Discrimination

Groomr is committed to being a fair and inclusive platform. Neither owners nor groomers may refuse service, provide an inferior standard of service, or treat another user less favourably because of a protected characteristic under the Equality Act 2010.

6.1 Protected characteristics

The protected characteristics under the Equality Act 2010 are: age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage and civil partnership, pregnancy and maternity, race, religion or belief, sex, and sexual orientation. Both direct discrimination (treating someone worse because of a characteristic) and indirect discrimination (applying a rule that disproportionately disadvantages a group) are prohibited.

6.2 Legitimate operational refusals

Groomers may decline a booking for legitimate operational reasons. These include:

  • The booking location falls outside the groomer's stated travel radius.
  • The dog's size or breed is outside the groomer's equipment capacity.
  • No available slots exist on the requested date.
  • Animal welfare grounds (see Section 5.2).

Stating a legitimate operational reason does not automatically provide cover for a refusal that is, in substance, discriminatory. Groomr reserves the right to investigate any refusal that appears inconsistent with this Code.

6.3 Obligation on Groomr

Groomr's enforcement of this Code must itself be applied consistently and without bias. If you believe an enforcement decision has been applied to you in a way that reflects a protected characteristic, you may raise this through the appeals process in Section 11.

7. Off-Platform Payment Prohibition

Groomr facilitates booking and payment. Deliberately directing a booking or payment off the platform to avoid the Groomr commission is a breach of the Terms of Service.

7.1 What is prohibited

The following constitute a breach of this Code:

  • Sharing bank details or alternative payment links before or during a Groomr booking.
  • Arranging recurring or future appointments verbally or through external channels following an initial Groomr booking, with the intent of avoiding future platform bookings.
  • Instructing an owner to pay cash and not record the appointment on the platform.

7.2 What is permitted

Cash tips given by an owner to a groomer at the time of or after the groom are permitted and do not constitute off-platform payment. This prohibition is directed at deliberate circumvention of the booking system — not at incidental cash contact between owner and groomer.

Note: Groomers with Stripe Connect accounts are paid out directly through the platform. The prohibition is on avoiding the booking record — not on the mechanics of how Stripe transfers funds. If an owner pays off-platform and a problem arises, Groomr has no payment record and cannot facilitate a refund or formal dispute.

8. Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is prohibited on the Groomr platform for all users. Breach of any item in this section is a serious breach of the Terms of Service and will typically result in immediate account suspension pending investigation.

  • Impersonation — pretending to be another user, a Groomr team member, a regulatory authority, or any other person or organisation.
  • Platform lead generation — using Groomr to generate leads for other booking channels or to advertise services outside of Groomr.
  • Unauthorised account access — attempting to access, interfere with, or compromise another user's account or personal data.
  • Illegal, obscene, or harmful content — uploading, sharing, or transmitting content that is unlawful, obscene, threatening, or depicts or encourages animal cruelty.
  • Fraudulent bookings — making or accepting bookings with no intention of attending, or in order to manipulate availability, metrics, or rankings.
  • Unauthorised sharing of personal data — sharing another user's personal information (including contact details, location, or data shared within the platform) outside the platform without their consent.
  • Threatening or grossly offensive communications — sending threatening, grossly offensive, or false messages via the Groomr platform. Such communications may constitute a criminal offence under the Malicious Communications Act 1988 or section 127 of the Communications Act 2003.
  • Any conduct that undermines the safety, integrity, or trust of the platform — including conduct not explicitly covered above that breaches the spirit of this Code or Groomr's Terms of Service.

9. Reporting and How We Handle Reports

If you believe another user has breached this Code, you can report it to Groomr. All reports are taken seriously and acted on in accordance with the process below.

9.1 How to report

You can raise a conduct concern through the following channels:

  • Conduct matters (harassment, prohibited content, discrimination, listing inaccuracies) — email legal@groomr.uk.
  • Animal welfare concerns — email safety@groomr.uk.
  • Conduct linked to a service failure (e.g. groomer no-show, owner non-disclosure causing harm) — use the Groomr disputes pathway described in the Disputes Policy. A conduct report and a dispute can run in parallel.

9.2 What happens next

When Groomr receives a conduct report:

  • We will acknowledge your report within 2 working days.
  • We will aim to reach an outcome within 10 working days. Complex cases may take longer — we will keep you informed of progress.
  • Outcomes are at Groomr's discretion within the enforcement framework in Section 10.

9.3 Confidentiality

Groomr will not disclose the identity of the person who made a conduct report to the subject of that report without the reporter's consent, except where disclosure is required by law or is necessary to prevent a risk of serious harm.

10. Enforcement

Where Groomr determines that a user has breached this Code or the Terms of Service, we may take enforcement action. The appropriate action will depend on the nature, severity, and frequency of the conduct.

10.1 Graduated consequences

Groomr's standard approach to enforcement follows a graduated framework:

  • Written warning — for a first low-level breach, with a clear explanation of what was wrong and what is expected going forward.
  • Content removal — removal of a specific message, review, listing, or other content, without broader account action.
  • Temporary restriction — suspension of booking or messaging functionality for a defined period.
  • Account suspension — full suspension pending investigation, with written notification to the account holder.
  • Permanent deactivation — for serious, repeated, or irremediable breaches of the Terms of Service. Deactivation terminates the account and the right to use the platform.
  • Referral to authorities — for conduct constituting a criminal offence (including fraud, harassment, or animal cruelty) or giving rise to a statutory welfare obligation, Groomr may refer the matter to the police, animal welfare authorities, or other regulatory bodies.

10.2 Serious conduct

Groomr reserves the right to skip steps in the graduated framework where the severity of the conduct warrants it. This includes — but is not limited to — conduct involving animal cruelty, fraud, physical threats, criminal offences, or conduct that poses an immediate risk to another user or animal. In such cases, immediate suspension or permanent deactivation may be applied without prior warning.

Enforcement basis: All enforcement actions are grounded in a specific breach of this Code or the Terms of Service. Groomr does not take enforcement action on an arbitrary basis — where action is taken, we will identify the rule breached and communicate this to you in plain language.

11. Appeals

If you believe an enforcement decision made against your account was wrong, you have the right to appeal.

11.1 How to appeal

Submit your appeal by email to legal@groomr.uk, stating the decision you are appealing, the reasons you believe it was wrong, and any supporting evidence. Appeals must be submitted within 28 days of the enforcement decision being communicated to you.

11.2 Appeal process

Your appeal will be reviewed by a member of the Groomr team who was not involved in the original decision. We will:

  • Acknowledge your appeal within 2 working days.
  • Provide a written outcome within 10 working days, or notify you if more time is required.
  • Explain the outcome in plain language, including the reasoning behind it.

11.3 Limits on appeals

Some enforcement decisions may not be fully reversible while a law enforcement process or regulatory investigation is under way. Where Groomr has referred a matter to the police or to an animal welfare authority, we may be unable to reinstate an account until that process has concluded.

11.4 Further escalation

If you remain dissatisfied following an appeal, you may refer the matter to the alternative dispute resolution (ADR) scheme referenced in Groomr's Terms of Service. If you believe Groomr's enforcement conduct has itself caused you a financial loss, the complaints procedure in the Terms of Service sets out the escalation route.

12. Groomr's Own Standards

This Code applies to Groomr as well as to its users. We commit to the following standards in how we operate the platform.

  • Consistent and unbiased enforcement — we will apply this Code consistently. Enforcement decisions will not be influenced by a user's commercial relationship with Groomr or by any protected characteristic.
  • Responsible use of user data — we will not use access to messages, booking history, or other user data for any purpose outside our Privacy Policy.
  • No manipulation of rankings or reviews — we will not artificially inflate or suppress groomer rankings, review scores, or review visibility to favour any commercial arrangement. Ranking criteria are based on objective factors such as ratings and booking volume.
  • Plain-language communication — we will communicate enforcement decisions in clear, plain language, including what action has been taken and which rule was breached.
  • Transparency about changes — we will give reasonable notice of material changes to this Code. Continued use of the platform after any change takes effect constitutes acceptance of the updated terms.
