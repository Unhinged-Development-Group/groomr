1. About these Owner Terms

These Owner Terms apply when you use Groomr to book dog grooming services. They sit alongside our Platform Terms of Use, our Privacy Policy, and the booking details of each appointment you make. By making a booking, you confirm that you have read and accepted these Owner Terms.

Groomr is operated by Unhinged Development Group Ltd, trading as Groomr ("we", "us", "Groomr"). You can contact us at hello@groomr.uk for general queries or legal@groomr.uk for legal matters.

2. The Booking is with the Groomer, Not with Us

When you book a grooming appointment through Groomr, the contract for that grooming service is between you and the Groomer who will perform it. Groomr is not a party to that contract. We provide the technology that allows you to find Groomers, view their services, book appointments, and pay deposits or full prices. We do not provide the grooming itself, employ the Groomers, or supervise the appointments.

This means: if your dog is injured, your appointment is unsatisfactory, or there is a dispute about the service, your contractual rights lie primarily against the Groomer. Groomr will help mediate where we can (see section 9), and the Verified badge tells you we have reviewed certain documents (see our Groomer Verification Policy), but the Groomer is the legally responsible party for the grooming.

3. Making a Booking

3.1 Creating an account

To make a booking, you need an account. You must be 18 or over and resident in the United Kingdom. The information you give us — including details about your dog (name, breed, age, size, temperament, and any relevant health notes) — must be accurate. If a Groomer cannot safely or competently groom your dog because you have given inaccurate information, the Groomer may refuse the appointment and you may forfeit any deposit.

3.2 When a booking is formed

A booking becomes a binding contract between you and the Groomer when you complete the booking flow on the Platform and the system displays a confirmation screen. We will also send a confirmation email. The terms of the booking are those displayed at the point of confirmation, including the service, the date and time, the price, any deposit, and the Groomer's cancellation policy.

3.3 What you are responsible for

As an Owner, you agree that you will:

  • provide accurate information about your dog;
  • declare any known behavioural issues, medical conditions, infectious illnesses, parasites, or recent injuries;
  • ensure your dog is up to date with core vaccinations unless an exemption is recorded with the Groomer;
  • arrive at the appointment on time, or notify the Groomer promptly if you are running late;
  • pay any balance owed at the end of the appointment in accordance with section 5; and
  • treat the Groomer and their premises with respect.

If you do not declare a relevant matter and the Groomer suffers loss or injury as a result (or refuses the service on discovering it), you may be liable to the Groomer and you may lose your deposit.

4. Deposits, Payment and Pricing

4.1 How prices are set

Each Groomer sets their own prices for their services. The price displayed on the Platform at the time you confirm the booking is the price you agree to pay, including any mandatory fees. Prices are inclusive of VAT where applicable. The Platform will show you the full booking price and any deposit required before you confirm.

4.2 The three deposit tiers

Each Groomer chooses one of three deposit policies, which will be displayed on their profile and at the point of booking:

  • No Deposit — payment is made in full to the Groomer at the appointment.
  • Fixed Deposit — a deposit (a percentage or fixed amount) is taken at the time of booking. The balance is paid to the Groomer at the appointment.
  • Full Prepayment — the full price is taken at the time of booking.

4.3 How payments are processed and held

Payments made on the Platform are processed by Stripe Payments Europe Ltd through Stripe Connect. When you pay a deposit or full prepayment, the funds are held by Stripe in connection with the Groomer's connected account and are released to the Groomer after the appointment has been marked as completed (typically within [24–48] hours of the appointment end time). 5. Cancellations, Rescheduling and No-Shows

5.1 Your statutory cancellation right (Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013)

Where you book a service that is not for a specific date within the next 14 days, you have a statutory right to cancel within 14 days of making the booking and receive a full refund, in accordance with the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013. To cancel under this right, email legal@groomr.uk within the 14-day period quoting your booking reference.

Important exception: if you book an appointment that takes place within the 14-day cancellation window, you expressly agree, by ticking the relevant box at checkout, that the service may be performed during the cancellation period. In that case you will lose the statutory right of cancellation once the service has been performed, or you may be required to pay for any service supplied before you cancel, in proportion to the value of the service delivered. The Groomer's commercial cancellation policy (set out in 5.2) applies in addition.

5.2 The Groomer's cancellation policy

Each Groomer sets a commercial cancellation policy, which is displayed on their profile and at booking. A typical structure is:

  • Cancellation more than 48 hours before the appointment: full refund of any deposit or prepayment, less any unrecoverable payment processing fees;
  • Cancellation between 24 and 48 hours before: deposit forfeited; prepayments refunded less the deposit equivalent;
  • Cancellation less than 24 hours before, or no-show on the day: deposit or full prepayment forfeited.

The exact times and amounts will be those displayed on the booking. Where the Groomer's policy is more generous (for example, free cancellation up to 24 hours), that policy applies. [REVIEW: forfeiture rules must comply with the Consumer Rights Act 2015 — a forfeiture that goes beyond a genuine pre-estimate of loss may be challenged as an unfair term.]

5.3 Rescheduling

You can usually reschedule a booking through your account, subject to the Groomer's availability. Rescheduling more than 48 hours before the appointment will not normally result in any loss of deposit. Rescheduling within 48 hours may be treated as a cancellation under section 5.2 at the Groomer's discretion.

5.4 Cancellation by the Groomer

If the Groomer cancels your booking (other than as a result of a breach by you of these Terms), you are entitled to a full refund of any deposit or prepayment. We will also help you find an alternative Groomer where possible.

5.5 Refunds

Refunds are processed back to the original payment method used at booking. We aim to issue refunds within 5–10 working days, although your card issuer may take longer to credit the funds to your account.

6. At the Appointment

6.1 Welfare

All Groomers on the Platform commit at registration to operate in compliance with the Animal Welfare Act 2006 (and the Animal Welfare (Scotland) Act 2006), and to uphold the Five Freedoms. If you observe conduct that you reasonably believe falls below these standards, you should remove your dog from the appointment and report the matter to us at safety@groomr.uk.

6.2 Discretion of the Groomer

A Groomer may, in their professional judgment, refuse to begin or stop a service if they believe the dog is unsafe to handle, unwell, in pain, or that the requested service is not in the dog's best interest. In such cases, the Groomer may charge a reasonable handling fee. Their reasons for the refusal will be communicated to you at the appointment or shortly afterwards.

6.3 Late arrival

If you arrive late, the Groomer may shorten or refuse the appointment. If they refuse on the basis of your late arrival, the appointment will normally be treated as a no-show under section 5.2.

7. Consumer Rights and Service Standards

The grooming service you book is supplied by the Groomer. Under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, the Groomer must:

  • perform the service with reasonable care and skill;
  • perform it within a reasonable time (where not pre-agreed);
  • charge a reasonable price (where not pre-agreed); and
  • match any description of the service given to you.

If a Groomer fails to meet these standards, you may be entitled to a repeat performance or to a price reduction. These rights are owed to you by the Groomer.

8. Reviews

After a completed appointment, you may leave a review of the Groomer. Reviews must be honest, based on your actual experience, and not defamatory or abusive. You may not be paid (in money or in kind) to leave a review. You may not threaten a Groomer with a negative review to obtain a refund or other benefit.

Groomr may moderate reviews to remove content that is defamatory, abusive, off-topic, contains personal data of others, or breaches these Terms. We will not remove a review simply because the Groomer disagrees with it.

9. Disputes With a Groomer

9.1 Try direct first

If you have a problem with a service (for example, the service was not what you booked, or your dog was returned in a different condition than expected), please first contact the Groomer through the Platform messaging system within 7 days of the appointment. Most issues can be resolved between the Owner and the Groomer.

9.2 Raise a dispute with Groomr

If you cannot resolve the matter with the Groomer, you may raise a formal dispute through the Platform (or by emailing disputes@groomr.uk) within 14 days of the appointment. You will need to describe the problem, provide any relevant evidence (photographs, messages, receipts), and tell us what outcome you are seeking.

Groomr will review the dispute and contact both parties. We will aim to acknowledge disputes within 2 working days and conclude review within 14 working days. Where appropriate, we may:

  • recommend a refund of the deposit (in part or in full);
  • recommend a price reduction or repeat service by the Groomer;
  • temporarily withhold the release of funds from the Groomer pending the outcome;
  • suspend the Groomer's account pending investigation; or
  • close the dispute without further action if the complaint is not substantiated.

9.3 Limits of our role

Groomr is not a court or a regulator. We can mediate and we control the booking funds while they are held by Stripe, but we cannot determine legal liability between you and the Groomer. If a dispute cannot be resolved through our process, you retain the right to pursue the Groomer through the courts or to make a claim through your payment provider's chargeback process.

9.4 Serious matters — injury or welfare

If the dispute concerns an injury to your dog or a welfare concern, please report it to us as soon as possible at safety@groomr.uk. Where appropriate, we may suspend the Groomer's profile pending investigation and may report the matter to the relevant local authority or the RSPCA / SSPCA.
