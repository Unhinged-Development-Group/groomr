1. About This Policy

This policy sets out the benefits available to employees of Unhinged Development Group Ltd (trading as Groomr), beyond the statutory floor that the law requires all employers to provide. It applies to all employees. Where benefits differ between those available from day one and those available after probation, this is stated clearly.

This policy does not apply to independent groomers or contractors on the Groomr platform, who are not employees of the company.

Benefits stated in an employment contract create binding entitlements — this policy aligns with and explains what your contract summarises. Part-time and fixed-term employees receive comparable benefits on a pro-rata basis in accordance with the Part-Time Workers Regulations 2000 and the Fixed-Term Employees Regulations 2002.

This policy is governed by Scots law. Employment claims arising under it fall within the jurisdiction of Employment Tribunals Scotland.

5. Cycle to Work Scheme

The company participates in an HMRC-approved Cycle to Work salary sacrifice scheme. This allows you to obtain a bicycle and cycling equipment in a tax-efficient way, with the cost spread across your salary over a defined period.

The scheme is available to all employees who are paid above the National Minimum Wage after the salary sacrifice deduction. Details of the current scheme provider and how to apply are available from the Founder.

Salary sacrifice arrangements reduce your gross pay for the period of the scheme, which may have a minor effect on pension contributions and certain state benefit calculations. Ask for guidance before committing if this concerns you.

6. Employee Assistance Programme

The company provides access to an Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) for all employees. The EAP offers free, confidential counselling and support — including mental health support, financial guidance, and legal information — available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

The EAP is entirely confidential. The company does not receive details of individual usage.

Access details and the contact number for the EAP are provided to employees on joining. If you cannot locate them, contact the Founder.

The EAP is an important resource. If you are experiencing difficulty — whether personal or work-related — we encourage you to use it without hesitation.

7. Equipment & Home Working

The company provides a laptop and necessary peripherals to all employees from day one. You do not need to provide your own equipment.

The full terms governing company equipment — acceptable use, security requirements, and return on leaving — are set out in the Use of Company Equipment Policy.

The company also pays a home-working allowance to reflect costs incurred when working from home. The amount and payment method are set out in the Remote Working Policy.

8. Other Perks & Benefits

The company may offer informal perks from time to time — such as team social events, flexible working arrangements, and other small benefits. These are discretionary and may change. Informal perks are not contractual unless they are expressly stated in your employment contract.

Benefits the company does not currently offer but may introduce in future:

  • Life assurance / death in service — not in place at current headcount, to be introduced as the team grows.
  • EMI share options — not currently offered. If introduced, scheme rules will be documented separately.

Personal development funding is covered in the Personal Development Policy. Expense reimbursement is covered in the Expenses Policy.
