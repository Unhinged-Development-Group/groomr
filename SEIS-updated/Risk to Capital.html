Risk to Capital Condition

01

Purpose of this document

This document has been prepared by Groomr Ltd to demonstrate that the proposed £10,000 seed investment satisfies the Risk to Capital condition as set out in section 257AAA of the Income Tax Act 2007, as it applies to the Seed Enterprise Investment Scheme (SEIS).

The Risk to Capital condition requires that, at the time of investment, there is a genuine risk the investor will lose more capital than they receive as a return. It further requires that the company intends to grow and develop its trade over the long term, and that this growth is intended to be permanent — not contingent on the continued financial support of the investor.

The two limbs of the condition are addressed in turn below, supported by evidence drawn from Groomr's business plan, financial projections, and operating structure.

The two limbs of the condition

Limb 1 — Genuine risk to capital: There must be a realistic possibility that the investor loses more than they receive as a net return. This is assessed at the time of investment, not with hindsight.

Limb 2 — Long-term growth intention: The company must intend to grow and develop its trade over the long term. That growth must be intended to be permanent and must not depend on the investor's continued financial support to be maintained.

02

Limb 1 — Genuine risk to capital

Groomr is a pre-revenue, early-stage marketplace business. At the time of this investment, the platform has been built and is in the process of launching into its first markets. No revenue has been generated. No customers have transacted. The commission-based marketplace model has been validated at scale by an existing competitor. However, Groomr's specific execution of that model — in its target markets, with its groomer network, and under its own brand — has not yet been tested. The risk lies in Groomr's ability to compete for that validated demand, not in whether the demand exists.

The following factors individually and collectively constitute a genuine risk that the investor will lose some or all of their capital:

Pre-revenue status

Groomr has not generated trading revenue at the date of this investment. The business is entirely dependent on successfully onboarding groomers and converting that supply into bookings. Failure at either stage would result in the loss of invested capital.

No guaranteed customer demand

Whilst market research and competitor evidence support the existence of demand, there is no guarantee that dog owners or groomers in the launch markets will adopt the platform at a rate sufficient to reach sustainability.

Competitive risk

Tuft, a funded competitor with an established user base, operates in the same market. A more aggressive competitive response — including price reductions or feature parity — could materially reduce Groomr's ability to grow its groomer network.

Execution risk

Groomr is a founder-led, single-operator business at launch stage. The platform's growth is dependent on the founder's ability to execute across technology, operations, marketing, and groomer relations simultaneously and without significant additional resource.

Runway risk

The £10,000 investment represents the primary source of early working capital. If the business does not reach commission revenue before this capital is exhausted, additional funding would be required or the business would cease to operate.

No asset backing

Groomr holds no significant tangible assets. The value of the business is entirely in its platform, brand, and groomer relationships — none of which carry recoverable value in a wind-down scenario. There is no asset base against which investor capital could be recovered.

Assessment

The combination of pre-revenue status, single-operator execution risk, no asset backing, and direct competition from a funded incumbent constitutes a genuine and material risk to capital at the time of investment. An investor could realistically lose the entirety of the amount invested. The SEIS income tax relief and loss relief provisions exist precisely to mitigate risks of this nature.

03

Limb 2 — Long-term growth intention

Groomr is being built with the explicit and documented intention of growing its trade permanently and substantially over a multi-year horizon. This intention is not incidental to the business model — it is its defining characteristic. A marketplace only generates value as it scales. A platform serving 10 groomers is not commercially viable. A platform serving 1,000 groomers across the UK generates meaningful revenue, network effects, and brand equity.

The following demonstrates that Groomr's growth intention is genuine, structured, and independent of this individual investment.

Documented growth trajectory

Groomr's business plan, included in the SEIS application pack, sets out a phased national expansion strategy. Year 1 targets 150 active groomers across Glasgow and Edinburgh. Year 2 expands to 400 groomers with entry into additional Scottish and Northern English cities. Year 3 targets 900 groomers and the introduction of a groomer subscription tier, affiliate revenue, and a premium owner membership — diversifying revenue beyond commission alone.

Year 1

Establish supply and validate the model

Target 150 active groomers in Glasgow and Edinburgh. Validate commission-based revenue. Build verified groomer profiles and owner booking behaviour. Revenue projected at approximately £29,000 from commission and onboarding fees.

Year 2

Expand geographically and deepen the product

Scale to 400 active groomers. Expand into additional cities. Introduce groomer subscription tier providing advanced tools and analytics. Begin affiliate partnerships with pet insurance and product providers. Revenue projected at approximately £304,000.

Year 3

National presence and revenue diversification

Scale to 900 active groomers. Establish Groomr as the recognised national marketplace for independent UK dog groomers. Revenue projected at approximately £1,930,000 across commission, subscription, and affiliate income streams.

Year 4+

Sustained independent operation

By Year 3 the business is projected to be operationally profitable on a monthly basis. Beyond Year 3, growth continues under its own commercial momentum — driven by organic search traffic, groomer referrals, and an established brand — without dependence on external capital injection.

04

Growth is intended to be permanent

HMRC requires that the growth and development of the business is intended to be permanent, and not contingent on the continued financial support of the investor. Groomr satisfies this requirement on the following basis:

Commission model
Self-funding
Revenue scales directly with platform usage — no external capital required to sustain operations once trading
SEO architecture
Compounding
Every groomer profile indexed by Google creates permanent, organic search presence that grows without paid spend
Network effects
Structural
Each groomer added improves owner choice; each owner booking validates the platform for the next groomer
Profitability target
Year 1
The business plan targets monthly operational profitability before the end of Year 1, reducing dependency on external capital

The £10,000 seed investment is intended to bridge the gap between launch and first commission revenue — a period of approximately three to six months. It is not a recurring dependency. The business model does not require ongoing investor support to sustain or grow the trade once the initial traction phase is complete.

Revenue diversification from Year 2 onwards — through groomer subscription fees and affiliate partnerships — further reduces reliance on any single income stream and strengthens the permanence of the business's commercial position.

Intended employment growth

Groomr launches as a founder-led business. The business plan anticipates the engagement of part-time contracted support from Year 2 as booking volume increases, with a view to full-time employment from Year 3 as the business scales nationally. The growth of the team is directly tied to the growth of the platform's commercial activity and is not dependent on the continued financial support of the seed investor.

05

Structures that would disqualify the condition

HMRC guidance identifies certain arrangements that would cause a company to fail the Risk to Capital condition, even where the investment appears on its face to be SEIS-eligible. Groomr confirms the following:

No guaranteed exit or return

No arrangement exists — formal or informal — that guarantees the investor a return of capital, a minimum return, or a right to require the company to buy back shares at any time.

No linked loans

The investment is not connected to any loan arrangement, either from the investor to the company or from the company to the investor. No debt instrument accompanies or underpins the share subscription.

No put options

No put option, call option, or other derivative arrangement exists that would allow the investor to require the company or any connected party to purchase their shares at a fixed or formula-determined price.

No tax-motivated structure

The investment has not been structured with the primary purpose of obtaining SEIS relief. The business plan, the commercial model, and the growth strategy exist independently of the tax relief framework.

Ordinary shares only

Shares issued are standard ordinary shares. No preference shares, redeemable shares, or shares carrying enhanced or preferential rights are being issued in connection with this investment.

No short-term exit planning

The company has no intention of winding down, ceasing to trade, or disposing of its assets in a manner that would return capital to investors within the SEIS qualifying period.

06

Summary

Groomr Ltd satisfies both limbs of the Risk to Capital condition as required by section 257AAA of the Income Tax Act 2007:

Limb 1
Genuine risk to capital is present

The investment is made into a pre-revenue, asset-light, founder-led business in a competitive market. There is a realistic and material possibility that the investor will lose some or all of the capital invested. No arrangement exists to protect, guarantee, or return that capital outside of the normal commercial performance of the business.

Limb 2
Long-term growth intention is genuine and permanent

Groomr is being built with a documented, multi-year growth plan targeting national scale. The business model is self-funding once trading commences. Growth is driven by compounding SEO, network effects, and revenue diversification — not by ongoing investor capital injection. Employment is expected to grow in line with commercial activity from Year 2 onwards.
