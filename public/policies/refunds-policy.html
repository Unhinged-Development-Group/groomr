1. How We Handle Payments

Groomr is a marketplace, not a service provider. When you book through Groomr, your service contract is with the individual groomer — not with Groomr. This shapes how payments, refunds, and liability work throughout this policy.

1.1 The contracting relationship

When a booking is confirmed through Groomr, a service contract is formed directly between the dog owner and the independent groomer. Groomr facilitates discovery, booking, and payment. It does not employ groomers, does not perform grooming services, and is not a party to the service contract.

1.2 Payment processing

All payments are processed by Stripe, our third-party payment processor. Groomr does not hold your money. When you pay for a booking, Stripe collects and holds the payment. At the point of payout, Stripe transfers the groomer's earnings to their connected Stripe Express account, with Groomr's platform fee deducted.

1.3 Payment flow

For a typical booking:

  • At booking: you pay either a deposit or the full service amount, depending on the groomer's settings. The amount is shown at the booking confirmation step before you commit.
  • For deposit bookings: the remaining balance is charged automatically once the groomer marks your appointment as complete in the Groomr app.
  • Groomer payout: Groomr pays out to groomers every Monday. The platform fee is deducted at the point of payout. Founding groomers pay a reduced rate during their founding period, confirmed at sign-up.

2. Deposits

Whether a deposit is required — and how much — is set individually by each groomer. The deposit amount is shown clearly at the booking confirmation step before you commit. You will not be charged a deposit without seeing it first.

A deposit is part of the total service price, not an additional charge. The total you pay across deposit and balance will never exceed the advertised service price.

2.1 What happens to your deposit if you cancel

This is governed by your groomer's terms and conditions. Before your first booking with any groomer, you will be asked to review and accept their terms. Those terms set out the cancellation windows and forfeiture rules that apply to all your bookings with that groomer.

All groomer terms on the Groomr platform must meet Groomr's minimum cancellation standards — no groomer may require forfeiture of a deposit for a cancellation made with sufficient notice. See section 4 for how cancellations work in practice.

3. Your Right to Cancel

By confirming a booking, you are waiving your 14-day statutory right to cancel. This is required because you are requesting the service start within that window. Your cancellation rights are then governed by the groomer's terms and this policy. Read this section carefully.

3.1 The 14-day statutory cooling-off period

Under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013, you ordinarily have 14 days to cancel a contract made at a distance — including an online booking — and receive a full refund without giving a reason.

3.2 How this applies to Groomr bookings

When you confirm a booking through Groomr, you are expressly asking us to arrange your appointment — which will fall within that 14-day window. This is stated clearly at the point of confirmation, and is recorded in your booking confirmation email. By completing the booking, you acknowledge that once the appointment has been carried out, your statutory right to cancel for a full refund no longer applies.

After the point of booking, your cancellation rights are governed by the groomer's terms and conditions and this policy — not by the 14-day statutory cooling-off period.

3.3 Your service rights are not affected

This waiver relates only to your right to cancel the booking for no reason. It does not affect your statutory rights in relation to the grooming service itself. If the service is not performed with reasonable care and skill, your rights under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 apply regardless of this waiver. See Groomr's Disputes Policy for how service quality issues are handled.

4. Cancellations

4.1 Cancelling as an owner

To cancel a booking, go to the relevant appointment in your Groomr dashboard and select cancel. The refund or deposit rules that apply depend on your groomer's individual terms and conditions, which you accepted before your first booking with them.

Groomr's platform standards require all groomer terms to permit full refunds for cancellations made with sufficient advance notice, and that any deposit forfeiture is limited to cancellations made close to the appointment where the groomer is unlikely to be able to rebook the slot. No groomer on the platform may impose cancellation charges that are disproportionate to their actual loss.

4.2 If your groomer cancels

If your groomer cancels your appointment, you will receive a full refund of everything you paid — including any deposit. No deductions will be made. Groomr will process the refund instruction within one business day of the cancellation being confirmed. You will be notified immediately by email and in-app.

Groomr absorbs the platform fee on groomer-cancelled bookings. You receive every penny you paid.

4.3 Group bookings

In a multi-dog group booking, each appointment is treated individually for cancellation purposes. Cancellation terms apply per appointment in accordance with the groomer's terms and conditions. You may cancel individual dogs from a group booking without affecting the others.

5. No-Shows

5.1 If you don't attend (owner no-show)

If you do not attend your appointment and have not cancelled in advance, the no-show terms in your groomer's terms and conditions apply. This typically means the full appointment fee is retained by the groomer. The groomer has blocked the time, cannot fill the slot at short notice, and has suffered a genuine loss.

5.2 Undisclosed health or welfare issues

If a groomer refuses to proceed with your appointment because your dog presents a welfare risk — such as aggression, illness, or out-of-date vaccination records — the financial outcome is governed by the groomer's terms and conditions. However, if you failed to disclose a known health or behavioural issue at the time of booking, you may be liable for the full appointment fee regardless of those terms. Owners are required to disclose known health and temperament issues at booking — see Groomr's Acceptable Use Policy for owner disclosure obligations.

5.3 If your groomer doesn't attend (groomer no-show)

If your groomer fails to attend your appointment, this is treated as a groomer cancellation. You will receive a full refund — see section 4.2.

5.4 Disputed no-shows

If there is a dispute about whether a no-show occurred, raise it through your Groomr dashboard or contact disputes@groomr.uk. See Groomr's Disputes Policy for full details on the process.

6. Refunds

6.1 Refund method

Refunds are always returned to the original payment method. We do not issue store credit or account credit by default. If you are offered credit as an alternative, accepting it is entirely voluntary — you are always entitled to a refund to your original payment method where a refund is owed.

Refunds take time to appear on your statement. Once Groomr instructs Stripe to process a refund, it typically takes 3–10 business days for the amount to appear on your card statement. This is controlled by Stripe and your card issuer — Groomr cannot accelerate it.

6.2 Groomer-cancelled appointments

If the groomer cancels your appointment, you receive a full refund. Groomr will process the refund instruction within one business day. Groomr absorbs the platform fee in these cases — you receive every penny you paid, not just the net amount after the fee.

6.3 Dispute-related refunds

Where a refund is agreed as part of Groomr's dispute resolution process, the amount will be as determined in the resolution outcome. The refund is processed to the original payment method with the same timescales as any other refund.

6.4 Partial refunds

Where a partial refund is agreed — for example, where a service was partially delivered — the refund is the agreed amount only, returned to the original payment method.

6.5 Tips

Tips are separate voluntary payments and are not refundable through this process. If you believe a tip was charged without your authorisation, contact hello@groomr.uk — this may be handled as a chargeback matter (see section 7).

7. Chargebacks

7.1 What a chargeback is

A chargeback is a payment dispute raised through your bank or card issuer. It is separate from Groomr's own refund and dispute processes, and is administered by Stripe and the relevant card scheme — not by Groomr. Groomr cannot determine the outcome of a chargeback.

7.2 What Groomr will do

If your bank raises a chargeback, Groomr will submit available evidence to Stripe — including booking records, confirmation emails, and platform communications — to assist in the resolution process. We cannot guarantee outcomes. Where the evidence clearly supports the validity of the charge, we will say so.

7.3 Running a chargeback alongside a Groomr process

Running a chargeback and a Groomr refund or dispute request at the same time can complicate and delay both. We recommend using Groomr's own refund or dispute process in the first instance — these are typically faster and within Groomr's control.

7.4 For groomers — chargeback impact

Where a chargeback succeeds, Stripe will debit the chargeback amount from your connected Stripe Express account, plus any dispute fee charged by Stripe. This is standard Stripe Connect behaviour — Groomr has no discretion over it. If you receive notice of a chargeback for an appointment you believe was legitimately completed, contact disputes@groomr.uk as soon as possible. Timely evidence submission through Stripe improves outcomes.

8. Groomer Payouts, Fees & Clawback

This section applies to groomers. It covers when and how you receive your earnings, the platform fee that applies, and what happens if Groomr needs to recover a refund that has already been paid out to you.

8.1 Payout schedule

Groomr pays out weekly, every Monday. Earnings from appointments marked as complete in the preceding week are included in the Monday transfer to your Stripe Express account. Stripe then transfers those funds to your bank account on its standard schedule, visible in your Stripe Express dashboard.

You must have a connected and verified Stripe Express account to receive payouts. Earnings from appointments completed before your account is verified will be held until verification is complete.

8.2 Platform fee

Groomr deducts a platform fee from each payout. The current standard rate is 8%. Founding groomers pay 0% during their founding period — your founding period end date was confirmed in your sign-up email. Once the founding period ends, the standard rate applies automatically.

If Groomr changes the platform fee rate, you will receive at least 30 days' written notice.

8.3 Clawback

If Groomr issues a refund to an owner after your earnings for that appointment have already been paid out, Groomr is entitled to recover the net amount paid to you. This section explains how recovery works.

Where Groomr refunds an owner for an appointment that has already been included in a Monday payout, Groomr is entitled to recover the net amount you received for that appointment (the booking total less the platform fee). Recovery is applied in this order:

  • Stripe balance offset. Stripe automatically debits your connected Express account for the clawback amount. This happens without any action required from you and cannot be prevented — it is a standard feature of Stripe Connect destination charges.
  • Future payout offset. If your Stripe balance is insufficient to cover the clawback in full, the outstanding amount will be deducted from the following Monday's payout. Groomr will notify you by email when this applies, showing the amount withheld and the specific refund it relates to.
  • Direct recovery. If you close your account or leave the platform with an outstanding clawback balance, Groomr reserves the right to recover that balance as a debt. By using the platform, you acknowledge that any unpaid clawback balance is a liquid debt immediately due to Groomr, enforceable through the Sheriff Court in Scotland.

8.4 Disputing a clawback

If you believe a refund was issued in error — and that the resulting clawback should not have arisen — contact disputes@groomr.uk within 14 days of being notified of the clawback, explaining why you believe the underlying refund was incorrect. Raising a dispute does not suspend the clawback — payout offsets will continue while the review is in progress. If your dispute is upheld, the withheld amount will be restored in your next Monday payout.

8.5 When clawback does not apply

Where a refund is issued for an appointment that has not yet reached payout — typically because the appointment was cancelled before it took place — there is nothing to claw back. Stripe refunds directly from the held payment. The clawback mechanism applies only where a refund is issued after the appointment was included in a Monday payout.

Where you cancel an appointment and Groomr refunds the owner in full, Groomr absorbs the 8% platform fee. You are not clawed back for the fee portion on your own cancellations.

8.6 Chargebacks

Where a clawback arises from a card-scheme chargeback rather than a Groomr-adjudicated refund, the outcome is determined by Stripe and the card scheme — Groomr has no discretion. You bear the full chargeback amount plus any Stripe dispute fee charged to Groomr's account in connection with the dispute. See section 7.4 for guidance on responding to chargebacks.

9. Disputes & Complaints

9.1 Payment disputes

If you believe a charge, refund, or payout is incorrect, contact disputes@groomr.uk within 30 days of the relevant appointment date. Include your booking reference, the amount in question, and a brief description of the issue. We aim to respond within 5 business days, and to resolve or escalate within 10 business days.

9.2 Service disputes

If your dispute concerns the quality of a grooming service rather than a payment, the process is governed by Groomr's Disputes Policy. Service quality disputes are between the owner and the groomer — Groomr is not a party to the service contract and does not adjudicate on service quality. Groomr can facilitate communication and, where appropriate, assist in reaching a resolution.

9.3 Escalation — alternative dispute resolution

If a payment dispute cannot be resolved through Groomr's internal process, you may refer it to Ombudsman Services as a certified alternative dispute resolution provider under the Alternative Dispute Resolution for Consumer Disputes Regulations 2015. Groomr will cooperate fully with any ADR process. Information is available at ombudsman-services.org.

9.4 Court proceedings

Nothing in this policy limits your right to bring a legal claim. For lower-value claims in Scotland (broadly up to £5,000), Simple Procedure in the Sheriff Court provides a straightforward route without the need for a solicitor — see scotcourts.gov.uk. For claims in England and Wales, the small claims track of the County Court applies — see gov.uk/make-court-claim-for-money. For Northern Ireland, contact the Northern Ireland Courts and Tribunals Service at justice-ni.gov.uk.

9.5 Governing law

This policy is governed by Scots law. Unhinged Development Group Ltd is a Scottish-registered company and the Scottish courts — the Sheriff Court and Court of Session — have jurisdiction over claims brought against Groomr as a Scottish entity. This does not override your statutory consumer rights or your ability to bring a claim in the courts of your own jurisdiction if you are based in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland. Most contractual claims in Scotland are subject to the five-year short negative prescription under the Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973.
