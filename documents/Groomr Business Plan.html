Executive Summary

Groomr is a UK-based online marketplace connecting dog owners with independent, local dog groomers. We exist to solve a genuine, frustrating, and surprisingly persistent problem: finding and booking a dog groomer in the UK is still a chaotic, fragmented process rooted in social media DMs and word-of-mouth — in 2026.

Our founder discovered this gap firsthand. Having moved several times across the UK with a dog in tow, the process of locating a new groomer meant trawling through Google results with no websites, hunting down Instagram profiles, and enduring back-and-forth messaging just to book an appointment. It is a poor customer experience that no modern service should accept.

Groomr changes that. We give pet owners a clean, intuitive platform to search for local groomers, view profiles with verified reviews and pricing, and book with confidence — complete with transparent deposit policies and instant confirmation. For groomers, we offer a professional digital presence and a dashboard to manage bookings, client records, messaging, and marketing without the chaos of Instagram Messenger.

The UK professional dog grooming market was valued at over £420 million in 2022 and is forecast to grow at approximately 5.7% annually.[3] Of an estimated 10,600 groomers in the UK,[4] the vast majority operate independently with no formal online booking system. 02

The Problem We're Solving

The problem at the heart of Groomr is not a niche frustration — it is structural to how the UK's independent grooming sector operates today.

The Pet Owner Experience

This is an entirely unnecessary barrier. Consumers already book taxis, hair appointments, and restaurant tables via clean, instant platforms.

When a dog owner moves to a new area — or simply needs a new groomer — the process looks remarkably like it did fifteen years ago:

  • • A Google search returns a handful of results, most leading to outdated or non-existent websites.
  • • The groomer's most recent activity is on Facebook or Instagram, requiring the owner to have an active social media account.
  • • Booking is done via direct message — introducing delays, ambiguity, and unnecessary back-and-forth.
  • • Pricing, availability, and groomer credentials are rarely surfaced upfront — everything requires a conversation.
  • • Confirmation is informal, often just a message thread, with no structured reminder or cancellation policy.

This is an entirely unnecessary barrier. Consumers already book taxis, hair appointments, and restaurant tables via clean, instant platforms. There is no logical reason grooming should be any different.

The Groomer Experience

Independent groomers are skilled professionals running small businesses, often solo, often from home. But the tools they rely on are not designed for them:

  • • Social media DMs mix personal and business communication, creating chaos and missed enquiries.
  • • There is no single view of bookings, clients, or revenue — it lives across notebooks, spreadsheets, and message threads.
  • • No-shows are common with no automated reminder or deposit mechanism in place.
  • • Word-of-mouth is the dominant acquisition channel, limiting growth and creating feast-or-famine booking cycles.
  • • Groomers who want a more professional presence must build their own website — a skill most lack and a cost many cannot justify.

Supply-Side Viability

The Administrative Burden

To succeed, a marketplace must solve problems for both the consumer and the supplier. While pet owners want seamless booking, independent groomers are desperate for comprehensive business management tools. Industry forums are filled with complaints regarding the highly fragmented method of managing a business across Instagram DMs, WhatsApp, SMS, and phone calls.

  • •
    Time Wasted: Independent groomers typically waste up to 24% of their working week on administrative tasks[8]. This translates to roughly 1 to 2 hours a day lost to manual scheduling and chasing payments — time that could be spent grooming an additional dog (equivalent to £45-£60 lost revenue daily).
  • •
    Burnout: The constant ping of DMs from clients arguing over slots, failing to read policies, or cancelling last minute leads to severe groomer burnout. They want to be with dogs, not acting as full-time receptionists.

The B2B SaaS Opportunity

There is immense, validated demand from groomers for an all-in-one, modern web platform. B2B software like MoeGo, Gingr, and Groomsoft prove that groomers will actively use (and pay for) comprehensive back-end tools.

By offering a web-based ecosystem that includes fully automated booking, integrated financials, centralised messaging, detailed pet profiles, and review management, we are not just providing a "marketplace listing."

We are providing a Business-in-a-Box.

If you can provide these essential B2B tools while simultaneously driving net-new clients to them via the consumer marketplace, the value proposition to the groomer becomes undeniable.

Market Opportunity

Market Size

The UK pet grooming services market was valued at approximately USD 562.9 million in 2024 and is projected to reach USD 804.8 million by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 6.4%.[6] Dogs account for over 87% of this revenue.

An estimated 12 million dogs live in the UK. Some 30% of dog owners visit a grooming parlour in any given year, while 84% arrange at least one professional groom annually.[7] With approximately 10,600 groomers serving this demand — the majority independent, the majority without formal online booking — the infrastructure gap is obvious.

Structural Tailwinds

  • • Pet humanisation: UK pet owners increasingly treat animals as family members, spending more on premium professional care.
  • • Post-pandemic growth: Pet ownership surged during COVID-19 and has not reversed, creating a persistently larger customer base.

Deep Dive: Tuft App

Tuft is the primary direct competitor in the UK, offering both consumer and groomer apps.

  • •
    Scale: Founded in 2021, raised ~£1.5M - £2M, reported £3.5m GMV in 2024 with over 83,000 active users.[9]
  • •
    Pain Points & Brand Weaknesses:
    • App-Only Friction: Tuft uses separated native apps instead of web, creating massive top-of-funnel drop-off for discovering local groomers.
    • The "Request" Hurdle: Despite marketing "instant booking," many appointments still require manual groomer approval.
    • Consumer Fee Backlash: Pet owners are noticeably frustrated by the £1.25 "Platform Fee" added to their checkout just for booking a service.
    • Prohibitive B2B Pricing: Their £32.99/month (or £46+ for multi-staff) subscription is difficult to justify for brand-new or small-scale independent groomers compared to a commission-only model.
    • Corporate Branding Focus: Their brand identity feels very "commercial tech startup" rather than a warm, localised, community-driven pet service.

Strategic Opportunity

Their rapid £3.5m GMV growth proves immense consumer demand exists in spite of these frustrating UI/UX barriers and fees. A frictionless, web-based platform with community-centric branding and a fairer pricing model could rapidly acquire their dissatisfied users.

Indirect Competitors (SaaS)

GroomerBooker, Reservio, MoeGo, SmartBooker: These provide phenomenal backend scheduling software for groomers.

Weakness: They are not consumer discovery marketplaces. A consumer cannot go to "MoeGo.com" to search for local groomers. Groomers must still do all their own marketing to get clients to use their personal booking links.

Tangential Competitors

Rover / Wag! / PetBacker: Massive marketplaces, but primarily focused on dog walking and sitting.

Weakness: While some groomers might list there, grooming is an afterthought on these platforms, lacking the specialised tools required to properly service the industry.

Business Model

Groomr generates revenue by facilitating high-trust connections. Our revenue is structured to scale directly with the success of the groomers on our platform.

Core Revenue Streams

Revenue Stream Strategy
All-Inclusive Commission (Year 1) Flat 8% fee on each new booking. Transparent, no hidden math, simple for the groomer.
Payment Processing Arbitrage Capturing the margin between gateway costs (e.g. Stripe 1.2%) and our all-inclusive rate. Invisible high-margin revenue.
Groomer Subscription (Year 2+) £14.99/mo premium tier for established groomers wanting advanced reporting & reduced 5% commissions.
Promotional Placements (Year 2+) Visibility boosts for groomers wanting to appear at the top of local search results.

Future Revenue Possibilities

As the platform scales into Phase 3 and beyond, our concentrated user base unlocks high-margin ancillary, affiliate, and referral opportunities within the broader pet care ecosystem:

Affiliate Commissions

% cut from retail and professional equipment sales (clippers, shampoos) made via a future dashboard shop.

Referral Bounties

Fixed lead-gen fees negotiated with pet insurance providers and vets for referring breed-specific customers.

Sponsored Content & Data

Direct-to-professional marketing fees and anonymised trend data for large pet supply brands.

Platform Share Philosophy

Groomr does not impose exclusivity on groomers. There is no contractual requirement to route all bookings through the platform. Instead, we earn platform share organically — by building a product so frictionless and valuable that groomers actively prefer it over manual booking management. Once a groomer sees Groomr handling reminders, deposits, client records, and confirmations automatically, managing bookings off-platform starts to feel like unnecessary extra work.

This approach builds trust and goodwill with the supply side at the critical onboarding stage, and avoids the adversarial dynamic that hard exclusivity clauses create in early-stage marketplace businesses.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Groomr's go-to-market strategy is deliberately local-first. Rather than a thin national launch, we build concentrated, high-quality presence in a small number of seed markets before expanding.

Phase 1: Seed Markets

Months 1–6

  • • Select 2–3 UK cities with high independent groomer density and active dog ownership.
  • • Founder-led direct groomer outreach: in-person visits, local grooming Facebook groups, industry forums.
  • • Founding Groomer Incentive: any groomer who signs up within the first 3 months receives zero commission for 6 months from their individual sign-up date. This is a time-limited, clearly communicated launch offer.
  • • Consumer-side demand seeded through targeted local digital advertising and dog owner community channels.

Phase 2: Regional Expansion

Months 6–18

  • • Expand to additional cities using seed market learnings.
  • • Introduce subscription tier for groomers who have seen tangible booking volume from the platform.
  • • Begin SEO investment to own 'dog groomer [city]' search terms nationally.
  • • Launch groomer referral incentives to accelerate supply-side growth.

Phase 3: National Scale

Month 18+

  • • National brand campaign to establish Groomr as the default dog grooming booking destination.
  • • Additional product features: breed-specific recommendations, mobile groomer listings, gift cards.
  • • Partnerships with pet insurance providers and veterinary chains for cross-referral.

Why Now?

The conditions for Groomr's success have never been better aligned:

  • • The UK's dog population is at a historic high, and owner spending on professional care continues to climb.
  • • Social media is declining as a preferred communication channel for commercial transactions, particularly among 25–44 year olds — the core demographic of dog owners.
  • • The UK grooming sector is actively calling for professionalisation. A trusted booking platform that surfaces verified, professional groomers supports this goal.
  • • Consumer expectations around digital booking have been permanently reset by the pandemic. Waiting two days for a DM reply is no longer acceptable.
  • • No dominant player has yet established category ownership in UK dog grooming booking. The window to become the default platform is open — but not indefinitely.

Groomr is the right product, for the right market, at the right moment.

Break-Even Analysis

At an 8% commission on a £55 average booking, Groomr earns £4.40 per transaction. At mid-range Year 1 costs of approximately £87,000, the platform must process roughly 19,775 bookings to break even.

Because groomers will route a conservative 60% of their bookings through the platform in Year 1 (~480 bookings/year per groomer), Groomr requires just 41 fully active groomers operating for a full 12-month period to break even on all Year 1 costs.

Subscription & Ancillary Revenue (Year 2+)

Year 2 transitions Groomr from a simple commission model into a diversified platform ecosystem, introducing two high-margin revenue streams.

The Subscription "No-Brainer"

Year 2 introduces the £14.99/month premium tier, which drops the platform commission from 8% to 5%. For a groomer routing a highly conservative 50 bookings a month through Groomr at £55, an 8% commission costs £220/month. A 5% commission costs £137.50/month. By paying £14.99, the groomer saves over £82 a month. Because the ROI is immediate and mathematically obvious, we project a 30% adoption rate in Year 2, generating ~£14,840 in pure SaaS ARR.

Ancillary Pet Ecosystem Revenue

By Year 2, Groomr holds concentrated, highly engaged relationships with hundreds of groomers and tens of thousands of pet owners. We conservatively project £45,000 in Year 2 (scaling to £140,000 in Year 3) generated via B2B affiliate links for professional grooming equipment, and B2C referral bounties (e.g., earning £50+ lead-gen fees by routing dog owners to partnered pet insurance providers post-booking).
