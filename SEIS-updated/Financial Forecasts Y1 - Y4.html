Financial Forecast 2026–2030

01

Purpose and basis of preparation

This document sets out Groomr Ltd's financial forecast for the four-year period from launch through to Year 4. It has been prepared to support the company's SEIS Advance Assurance application and to provide prospective investors with a transparent view of the financial model underpinning the business.

Five scenarios are modelled from significant underperformance to exceeding targets. Groomr Ltd has not yet commenced trading and no historical accounts exist. These forecasts represent the directors' best estimate of future performance based on available information at the time of preparation.

Important disclaimer

These projections are forward-looking statements based on assumptions that may not prove accurate. Actual results may differ materially. This document does not constitute financial advice. Prospective investors should conduct independent due diligence and seek professional financial advice before investing.

On salary structure

The founder operates on a low PAYE salary (£12,576/yr equivalent, pro-rata from Month 3 in Year 1) with additional remuneration via dividends from distributable profits. This is standard practice for UK owner-directors and is tax-efficient. From Year 3, PAYE salary increases to market rate as the business is established. Dividend projections are shown in Section 05 and are not a P&L cost. All salary figures in the cost model are gross PAYE — employer NI and pension are included on top.

08

Key assumptions and forecast risks

Assumption Basis Risk if wrong
49 bookings/month blended average Weighted across four groomer types: full-time established (30%), growing full-time (25%), part-time (30%), new/building (15%) If the platform skews toward part-time or new groomers, the blended average falls. Each 5-booking reduction in the average reduces annual revenue by approximately £13,000 per 100 active groomers.
100% of bookings through Groomr once onboarded Platform replaces all manual booking admin. No incentive to run parallel systems once migrated. Each 10% reduction in booking capture reduces net revenue ~10% across the board.
£55 average booking value Mid-range UK market estimate across breed sizes and service types. A £5 swing changes annual revenue by approximately £10,000 per 100 active groomers at the blended rate.
30% subscription adoption in Y2 Conservative — subscription saves £69/mo vs £14.99 cost at blended booking rate. Clear payback. At 15% adoption Y2 revenue reduces ~£17k. At 50% it increases by a similar amount.
Linear groomer ramp per year Simplified model. Actual growth will be slower early, faster later as referrals compound. Front-loaded slow adoption extends cash-positive timeline. Net annual figures unaffected.
Platform tier upgrade timing Free tier limits estimated conservatively at published thresholds. Faster growth could trigger upgrades earlier, increasing platform costs in Y1 by up to £77/month if all tiers upgrade simultaneously.
