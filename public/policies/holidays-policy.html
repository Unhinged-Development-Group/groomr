1. About This Policy

This policy sets out annual leave entitlement, how leave is requested and approved, bank holiday treatment, carry-over, and long-service leave for employees of Unhinged Development Group Ltd (trading as Groomr).

The statutory minimum entitlement under the Working Time Regulations 1998 is 5.6 weeks (28 days including bank holidays for a full-time employee). This policy confirms the company's entitlement, which exceeds the statutory minimum. The statutory floor cannot be contracted out of.

This policy applies to all employees. Part-time employees receive entitlement on a pro-rata basis. It does not apply to independent groomers or contractors on the Groomr platform.

This policy is governed by Scots law. Employment claims arising under it fall within the jurisdiction of Employment Tribunals Scotland.

2. Annual Leave Entitlement

All full-time employees are entitled to 32 days of paid annual leave per holiday year. This figure includes Scottish bank holidays — bank holidays are not granted in addition to the headline figure.

The 32-day entitlement exceeds the 28-day statutory minimum by 4 days. The additional 4 days are contractual. Different carry-over rules apply to the statutory 28 days versus the additional 4 contractual days — see section 6.

Employees who join or leave part-way through a holiday year accrue leave on a pro-rata basis. Your pro-rata entitlement for your first and final years will be calculated based on the number of complete months worked.

Holiday pay reflects your normal pay, including any regular overtime, commission, or allowances forming part of your normal working pattern, in line with Working Time Regulations case law.

3. Bank Holidays

Bank holidays are included within your 32-day annual leave entitlement — they are not granted on top of it. All employees follow the Scottish bank holiday calendar, which differs slightly from the English and Welsh calendar.

Scottish public holidays observed by the company (verify current year's dates via the Scottish Government website):

  • New Year's Day (1 January)
  • 2 January
  • Good Friday
  • Early May Bank Holiday
  • Spring Bank Holiday
  • Summer Bank Holiday (first Monday in August in Scotland)
  • St Andrew's Day (30 November)
  • Christmas Day (25 December)
  • Boxing Day (26 December)

Because bank holidays are included in the 32-day total, you do not need to take them off — you may work on a bank holiday and take a different day off in lieu if you prefer, subject to manager approval.

4. The Holiday Year

The company's holiday year runs from 1 January to 31 December each calendar year.

If you join or leave the company part-way through the year, your entitlement is calculated pro-rata based on the proportion of the holiday year you will have worked as an employee.

Holiday accrues during periods of statutory leave, including sickness absence, maternity, paternity, adoption, and shared parental leave. See the Sickness Policy and Parental Leave Policy for further detail on the interaction between leave types.

5. Booking Leave

All annual leave must be requested and approved in advance. Submit your leave request to your line manager with a minimum notice period of twice the length of the leave you are requesting — for example, two days' notice for one day's leave, ten days' notice for a five-day block.

The company will consider all leave requests fairly. Requests may be refused where operational requirements make approval impractical — for example, during a product launch or a critical deadline. Where a refusal is necessary, we will explain the reason and work with you to find an alternative time.

If the company requires you to take leave on specific dates (for example, an office closure), you will be given reasonable notice. Such requirements will not be used to disadvantage employees with particular religious or cultural observances — if a mandatory closure date conflicts with your observance, discuss it with your line manager and we will accommodate where possible.

The company may require you to use any outstanding annual leave during your notice period, in accordance with the Working Time Regulations 1998.

6. Carry-Over

Employees may carry over a maximum of 3 days of unused annual leave into the next holiday year. Carried-over leave must be used by 31 March of the following year or it is forfeited.

Carry-over above 3 days is not permitted in ordinary circumstances. Unused leave above this limit at the end of the holiday year is forfeited and will not be paid out.

Statutory carry-over rules apply in certain circumstances regardless of the above limit:

  • Long-term sickness: Where an employee is prevented from taking statutory leave due to illness, up to 4 weeks of statutory leave may be carried over for up to 18 months from the end of the leave year in which it accrued, in accordance with the Working Time (Amendment) Regulations 2023.
  • Family leave: Holiday accrued during maternity, adoption, or shared parental leave that cannot reasonably be taken before or after the leave period may be carried over by agreement.

7. Long-Service Sabbatical

In recognition of long service, employees who complete every 5 years of continuous employment with the company are entitled to a 4-week paid sabbatical.

The sabbatical is in addition to your standard annual leave entitlement. It must be taken within 12 months of becoming eligible — it does not carry over beyond that window and is not paid out on termination.

To take a sabbatical, submit a request to the Founder or a Director at least 3 months in advance. The timing of the sabbatical is subject to agreement based on operational requirements.

The sabbatical may be taken as a single 4-week block or split into two 2-week periods by mutual agreement. It cannot be split into individual days.

8. Leave on Termination

On termination of employment, any accrued but unused statutory annual leave (the 28-day minimum) will be paid to you in your final salary. The daily rate is calculated as your annual salary divided by 260 working days (or your pro-rata equivalent if part-time).

If you have taken more leave than you have accrued by the time your employment ends, the company may deduct the value of the excess days from your final pay, provided this is authorised by your employment contract.

The 4 additional contractual days above the statutory minimum are also paid on termination for any accrued but untaken entitlement in the current holiday year.

Long-service sabbatical entitlement that has not been taken is not paid out on termination.
