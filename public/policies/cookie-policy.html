1. About This Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Groomr uses cookies and similar technologies on the Groomr website at groomr.uk and our mobile applications (together, the "Platform"). It sits alongside our Privacy Policy, which describes how we handle personal data more generally.

Groomr is operated by Unhinged Development Group Ltd, trading as Groomr. Our data protection contact is reachable at privacy@groomr.uk.

2. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website. They allow the website to recognise your device, remember your preferences, and provide functionality and analytics. Cookies set by Groomr directly are called "first-party cookies"; cookies set by other domains (for example, Stripe or PostHog) are called "third-party cookies".

We also use technologies that are similar to cookies, such as local storage, session storage, web beacons, and pixels. Where this Policy refers to "cookies", it includes those technologies.

3. The Legal Position

We use cookies on the basis set out in the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR) and the UK General Data Protection Regulation:

  • Strictly necessary cookies do not require your consent. These are cookies that are essential for the Platform to work — for example, to keep you logged in or to process a payment. We rely on the PECR exemption for these.
  • All other cookies (analytics, marketing, preference) require your prior consent, which you give through our cookie banner when you first visit. You can withdraw consent at any time by reopening the cookie preferences from the link in our footer.

We do not place non-essential cookies until you have given consent. We use Cookiebot as our consent management platform; Cookiebot keeps a record of the consent you give and lets you change it at any time. [REVIEW: confirm vendor before launch.]

4.2 Analytics and product cookies (require consent)

These help us understand how the Platform is used so we can improve it. They are set only after you give consent through the cookie banner.

Cookie name Provider Purpose Duration
ph_*_posthog PostHog (eu.posthog.com) Identifies your browser to PostHog so we can analyse how the Platform is used, including page views, clicks, and feature usage (autocapture). 1 year
ph_session_id PostHog Identifies a single browsing session for PostHog session replay, which records cursor movements, clicks, scrolls and (masked) form interactions so we can see where users encounter problems. 30 minutes (rolling)
ph_session_recording PostHog Flag controlling whether the current session is being recorded for replay. Session

Session replay sensitive-input masking is enabled by default in our PostHog configuration. This means form fields containing personal information (such as your name, email, payment details, and free-text dog notes) are blocked from the recording. Even so, session replay involves a meaningful level of behavioural tracking, and we will not enable it unless you have given consent.

4.3 Marketing cookies (require consent)

At launch, Groomr does not use marketing or advertising cookies. We may introduce them in future — for example, a Meta (Facebook) Pixel or Google Ads conversion tag — to measure the effectiveness of campaigns and to show relevant ads. If and when we do, we will update this Policy, list the specific cookies here, and ask for your consent before any such cookie is placed on your device.

6. How to Control Cookies

6.1 Through the Groomr cookie banner

When you first visit the Platform, our cookie banner lets you accept all non-essential cookies, reject them, or pick specific categories. You can change your choice at any time by clicking "Cookie preferences" in the footer of any page on the Platform.

6.2 Through your browser

You can also manage cookies in your browser. Most browsers let you see what cookies are stored, delete some or all of them, and block cookies from being set in future. The following links give instructions for the major browsers:

  • Google Chrome — support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647
  • Mozilla Firefox — support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/clear-cookies-and-site-data-firefox
  • Apple Safari — support.apple.com/en-gb/guide/safari/sfri11471/mac
  • Microsoft Edge — support.microsoft.com/en-gb/microsoft-edge
  • Brave — support.brave.com/hc/en-us

Blocking strictly necessary cookies through your browser will affect Platform functionality — you may not be able to stay signed in, complete a booking, or use parts of the site. The Platform is not designed to work without those cookies.

6.3 Mobile devices

If you use the Groomr mobile application, you can manage tracking through your device settings (for example, "App Tracking Transparency" on iOS, or "Privacy" settings on Android).

6.4 "Do Not Track" signals

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" signal. There is no consistent industry interpretation of this signal, and UK law does not currently require us to act on it. We rely instead on the cookie banner and the choices you make there.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time — for example, when we add or remove a third-party service, or when an underlying library changes the cookies it sets. We will post the updated Policy on the Platform and update the version date below. Where the change is material, we will ask you to re-confirm your cookie preferences.
