1. About This Policy

This Acceptable Use Policy (“AUP”) sets out the standards of behaviour we expect from everyone who uses Groomr — dog owners, professional groomers, and anyone who simply visits the website. It applies to the Groomr website at groomr.uk, the Groomr mobile applications, and any related online services (together, the “Platform”).

This AUP forms part of our Platform Terms of Use, Owner Terms and Groomer Terms. By using the Platform, you agree to follow it. Breaking the rules in this AUP can lead to your content being removed, your account being suspended or terminated, your bookings being cancelled, and — in serious cases — us reporting you to the police, the Information Commissioner’s Office, the relevant local authority, or an animal welfare body such as the RSPCA or SSPCA.

Groomr is operated by Unhinged Development Group Ltd, trading as Groomr (“we”, “us”).

2. The Short Version

If you read nothing else, read this. When you use Groomr, you must:

  • treat animals with kindness and respect at all times;
  • treat other Groomr users (owners, groomers, our team) with respect;
  • be truthful in everything you say about yourself, your dog, or your business;
  • follow the law — including consumer law, animal welfare law, data protection law, and tax law as it applies to your activity on the Platform;
  • only upload content you have the right to upload;
  • not try to game, exploit, or circumvent how the Platform works.

The rest of this Policy explains each of these in more detail, with examples of what is and is not allowed.

3. Animal Welfare — the Non-Negotiable Bit

Groomr exists because dogs deserve to be groomed by professionals who care. Animal welfare is the single most important standard on the Platform, and any credible report of mistreatment is treated with the highest urgency.

3.1 What we expect from everyone

Whether you are a Groomer providing services or an Owner attending an appointment, you must:

  • comply with the Animal Welfare Act 2006 and (in Scotland) the Animal Welfare (Scotland) Act 2006;
  • uphold the Five Freedoms: freedom from hunger and thirst; from discomfort; from pain, injury and disease; to express normal behaviour; and from fear and distress;
  • not subject any dog to unnecessary suffering, restraint, or distress;
  • not use grooming techniques, products, or equipment that are known to cause pain, injury, or distress.

3.2 Specific to Groomers

  • Use appropriate handling techniques for the breed, age, temperament, and health of the dog in your charge.
  • Stop a service immediately if continuing would cause the dog distress, pain, or harm, and explain this to the Owner.
  • Refer dogs to a veterinarian where you observe a condition that requires veterinary attention (skin condition, injury, parasitic infestation, suspected medical issue).
  • Do not use forced-air drying near a dog’s face, harsh restraints, or any practice that puts welfare below convenience.
  • Maintain a clean, safe, well-ventilated working environment.

3.3 Specific to Owners

  • Be honest about your dog’s health, behaviour, and any aggression history at the point of booking.
  • Do not knowingly take a dog with an active parasitic infestation (fleas, mites, ticks), an open wound, or a contagious illness to a grooming appointment without prior agreement from the Groomer.
  • Pick up your dog promptly after the appointment.
  • Do not pressure a Groomer to perform a service that they have refused on welfare grounds.

3.4 Reporting welfare concerns

If you have a concern about how a dog has been treated on or in connection with the Platform, please report it to safety@groomr.uk immediately. We will investigate without delay. Where appropriate, we will suspend the account pending the outcome, refer the matter to the RSPCA or SSPCA, and cooperate with any local authority or police investigation.

4. Honesty and Identity

4.1 Be yourself

You must not:

  • create an account under a false identity or using someone else’s details;
  • impersonate another person or business, real or fictional;
  • claim qualifications, certifications, awards, or insurance you do not hold;
  • create more than one account per person or business, except where we have agreed otherwise in writing;
  • transfer, sell, or lend your account to anyone else.

4.2 Be honest about your dog (Owners)

When you book, you must declare:

  • the dog’s correct breed, age, and approximate size and weight;
  • any known health conditions, allergies, or recent veterinary treatment;
  • any history of biting, severe anxiety, or aggression towards humans or other dogs;
  • any matted coat, skin condition, parasitic infestation, or ongoing treatment that could affect the appointment;
  • whether your dog has experienced traumatic grooming sessions in the past.

Omitting any of these in a way that puts the Groomer or your dog at risk is a breach of this AUP and may result in loss of your deposit, refusal of service, or removal from the Platform.

4.3 Be honest about your business (Groomers)

Your listing must accurately reflect:

  • the qualifications and certifications you hold (no embellishments, expired credentials, or borrowed ones);
  • the insurance you carry;
  • your services, prices, opening hours, and availability;
  • the premises where the service is performed (in-salon, home, mobile);
  • the breeds and dog sizes you are competent and equipped to handle.

5. Content You Upload

This section covers photographs, profile pictures, listing copy, reviews, messages, and anything else you upload to or send through the Platform.

5.1 What you must not upload

You must not upload, post, or send any content that is:

  • unlawful, fraudulent, deceptive, or which promotes any illegal activity;
  • threatening, harassing, abusive, hateful, or which incites violence or discrimination on the basis of any protected characteristic under the Equality Act 2010;
  • defamatory of any person or business;
  • sexually explicit, pornographic, or otherwise inappropriate for a general audience;
  • graphically violent, gory, or designed to shock — including but not limited to images of severe injury, blood, or distressed animals presented for shock value;
  • infringing of any third party’s intellectual property rights (copyright, trade mark, design right, or similar);
  • a depiction of, or which promotes, animal cruelty or any conduct in breach of animal welfare law;
  • personal data about another person or their dog that you do not have the right to share (including photos that identify another person, vehicle registrations, addresses);
  • misleading or designed to deceive other users — for example, before-and-after photos that misrepresent your work;
  • spam, unsolicited advertising, chain-letter content, or promotional material for other businesses unrelated to your Groomr listing.

5.2 Specifically about dog photos

We expect Groomr to be a place where photos of dogs are joyful, professional, and welfare-respecting. Some specific rules:

  • Use your own photos, or photos you have explicit permission from the photographer and the dog’s owner to use. Owners retain rights in photos of their dogs.
  • Do not download or repost photos from social media, Pinterest, Google Images, or other groomers’ websites — these are not yours to use, and using them is both copyright infringement and misleading.
  • Show real work you have done. Stock photography is not acceptable for portfolio listings.
  • Do not photograph a dog who is visibly distressed, in pain, or restrained against their will.
  • Do not use photos that show a dog’s injury or wound in a way that is gratuitous or designed to shock.
  • Get the Owner’s permission before posting photos of their dog in your portfolio. The Owner can withdraw permission at any time and we will remove the photo on request.
  • Do not include identifying information about a dog’s owner in a photo caption (full name, address, registration plates in the background).

5.3 Profile photos

Your profile photo (for both Owners and Groomers) should be a real photograph of you or your business. Cartoons, memes, stock photos, photos of celebrities, and images of other people are not allowed. Groomer profile photos should be appropriate for a professional service business.

5.4 Our rights over content

We may, in our discretion, remove or refuse to publish any content that breaches this AUP, our Platform Terms, or applicable law. We do not pre-moderate every piece of content; we rely partly on user reporting and partly on automated tools to identify problems. If we remove your content, we will tell you why where it is reasonable and lawful to do so, and you can appeal by emailing legal@groomr.uk.

6. Reviews

6.1 Honest reviews only

Reviews are central to how trust works on Groomr. To keep them meaningful, you must:

  • only leave a review after a completed appointment that you (or your business) booked through Groomr;
  • be honest — reviews must reflect your actual experience;
  • be fair — review the appointment, not the Groomer’s personal characteristics;
  • not include personal data of any third party (other clients, employees, named individuals);
  • not include legal accusations you cannot substantiate.

6.2 Fake and incentivised reviews are illegal

Under the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024, fake reviews and incentivised reviews are illegal in the UK. You must not:

  • write a review of your own business (or have a friend, family member, employee or business associate write one);
  • pay anyone — in money, services, discounts, or anything else of value — to leave a review;
  • offer a discount or freebie in exchange for a positive review;
  • threaten or harass an Owner to remove a negative review;
  • write a negative review of a competitor;
  • commission reviews from third-party services.

We will remove fake or incentivised reviews when we find them, suspend the accounts involved, and may report serious or systematic cases to the Competition and Markets Authority.

6.3 Responding to reviews

Groomers may respond to reviews professionally. You must not threaten the reviewer, reveal personal information about them, make legal threats, or attempt to coerce them into changing or removing a review.

7. How You Talk to Other People on the Platform

Messages between Owners and Groomers are part of how bookings work. We expect them to be professional and respectful. You must not:

  • send abusive, threatening, harassing, sexually inappropriate, or discriminatory messages;
  • send unsolicited marketing or promotional messages;
  • share another user’s personal data without their consent;
  • attempt to recruit a Groomer’s clients to a competing business or to recruit an Owner’s pet for any unrelated commercial purpose;
  • encourage the other party to book or pay outside the Platform for services that originated through Groomr (see section 9);
  • contact other users outside the Platform for any purpose unrelated to a booking, unless that user has invited you to.

We may review messages where a dispute is raised or where we receive a report of misconduct. This is described in our Privacy Policy.

8. Safety of the Platform

You must not, and must not encourage anyone else to:

  • introduce viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware, or other malicious code;
  • attempt to gain unauthorised access to the Platform, any user account, any server, or any system associated with the Platform;
  • attempt to interfere with the proper working of the Platform (including denial-of-service attempts or rate-limit evasion);
  • probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of the Platform without our prior written permission;
  • circumvent or attempt to circumvent any security measure or access control;
  • reverse-engineer, decompile, or attempt to derive the source code of any part of the Platform, except where this is allowed by law and you have given us written notice;
  • scrape, crawl, copy, or extract data from the Platform by any automated means, except where we have given express written permission;
  • frame, mirror, or republish any part of the Platform without our written consent.

We welcome responsible security disclosure. If you find a vulnerability, please email security@groomr.uk with details and give us a reasonable time to fix the issue before publishing anything about it. We will not pursue legal action against good-faith researchers who follow this approach.

9. Don’t Try to Cut Groomr Out

If a booking originated through Groomr, the booking, the payment, and any rescheduling must go through Groomr. You must not:

  • encourage Owners to book or pay you off-platform (for example, “next time, just text me and we’ll save the fee”);
  • encourage Groomers to take you off-platform (for example, asking for their personal number to book directly);
  • use Groomr only to source leads and then move them to your own booking system.

This rule does not prevent a Groomer from accepting direct repeat bookings from clients who originally found them outside Groomr, or from running other booking channels in parallel. It also does not prevent Owners and Groomers from communicating about a current Groomr booking through ordinary channels (a confirmation call, a reminder text) where the Groomer offers that.

Repeat circumvention is a material breach and can result in account termination, forfeiture of pending payouts, and — in serious cases — legal action to recover lost commission.

10. Legal Compliance

By using the Platform you confirm that you will, in your activity on Groomr, comply with all law that applies to you. The most relevant areas are:

  • Animal welfare: Animal Welfare Act 2006 / Animal Welfare (Scotland) Act 2006.
  • Consumer law: Consumer Rights Act 2015; Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013; Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024.
  • Data protection: UK GDPR; Data Protection Act 2018; PECR.
  • Equality: Equality Act 2010 — do not discriminate against users on the basis of protected characteristics.
  • Tax: your own HMRC obligations as a self-employed individual or business.
  • Licensing: any local authority licence or registration that your business requires.
  • Health & safety: in respect of your premises, staff, and clients.

11. What Happens If You Break the Rules

11.1 How we decide what action to take

We aim to respond proportionately. The action we take will depend on factors including: how serious the breach is; whether it is a first-time issue or a pattern; whether anyone (human or canine) has been harmed; whether you cooperate with our investigation; and whether the conduct is unlawful.

11.2 The range of actions we may take

Depending on the circumstances, we may do any of the following:

  • remove the content or message in question;
  • require you to correct your profile or listing;
  • issue a warning;
  • temporarily limit features of your account (for example, prevent new listings, hide reviews, restrict messaging);
  • suspend your account pending investigation;
  • cancel pending bookings and direct refunds in accordance with our Terms;
  • withhold the release of funds held with Stripe pending the outcome of any dispute;
  • revoke or refuse the Verified badge;
  • permanently terminate your account and ban you from creating future accounts;
  • recover any losses we suffer as a result of the breach (including commission lost through off-platform circumvention);
  • report the matter to the police, the ICO, HMRC, the relevant local authority, the RSPCA / SSPCA, or any other appropriate authority or regulator;
  • cooperate with any criminal or civil investigation or proceedings.

11.3 Immediate action without notice

Where we reasonably believe there is a risk to animal welfare, to other users, or to the security of the Platform, we may take action immediately and without prior notice. Examples include credible reports of animal mistreatment, ongoing fraud, harassment of another user, or active security exploitation.

11.4 Appeals

If we take action against your account and you believe we have got it wrong, you may appeal by emailing legal@groomr.uk within 30 days, explaining why. We will review the decision, with a different person from the one who originally took the action where possible. We aim to respond to appeals within 14 working days. Our decision on appeal is final, but does not affect any rights you have under applicable law (including your right to complain to the ICO if the issue concerns personal data).

12. Reporting Breaches of This AUP

If you see something on Groomr that you think breaches this AUP, please tell us. The right contact depends on the issue:

  • Animal welfare or safety concern — safety@groomr.uk
  • A specific dispute about a booking you made — disputes@groomr.uk
  • Inappropriate content, fake reviews, or harassment — use the report button on the relevant profile or message, or email legal@groomr.uk
  • Security vulnerability — security@groomr.uk
  • Suspected fraud — legal@groomr.uk

We aim to acknowledge reports within 2 working days and act on them as soon as we reasonably can. Where you make a report, we will treat your identity confidentially unless we are legally required to disclose it, or unless disclosure is necessary to investigate the matter and you have agreed to it.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this AUP from time to time as we learn more about how Groomr is used and what kinds of conduct cause problems. We will post the updated AUP on the Platform and update the version date below. Where the change is material, we will tell you by email and through the Platform at least 30 days before it takes effect.
