1. About This Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Groomr collects, uses, shares and protects personal data when you use the Groomr website at groomr.uk, the Groomr mobile applications, and any related online services (together, the "Platform"). It applies to dog owners ("Owners"), professional groomers ("Groomers"), and anyone who simply visits the Platform.

Groomr is operated by Unhinged Development Group Ltd, trading as Groomr. We are the controller of the personal data described in this Policy unless we say otherwise. Our role as controller, the lawful bases we rely on, and your rights are set out in the sections below.

Please read this Policy alongside our Platform Terms of Use, Owner Terms, Groomer Terms, and Cookie Policy.

3. The Personal Data We Collect

We collect different categories of personal data depending on how you interact with the Platform. Most of it comes directly from you; some is generated automatically as you use the Platform; and a small amount is received from third parties (for example, payment confirmation from Stripe).

3.1 Data we collect from Owners

  • Identity and contact data: name, email address, phone number, postal address (used for finding nearby Groomers and for invoicing where applicable).
  • Account credentials: managed by our authentication provider, Clerk. We do not store your password.
  • Dog data: dog's name, breed, age, size, temperament notes, and any health or behavioural information you choose to share with a Groomer.
  • Booking data: appointments made, services selected, prices paid, deposit amounts, cancellations, and reviews you leave.
  • Payment data: limited payment metadata (last four digits of card, card type, transaction ID, payment status). Full card details are processed and stored by Stripe, not by us.
  • Communications: messages exchanged with Groomers through the Platform, and any emails you send us.

3.2 Data we collect from Groomers

  • Identity and contact data: name, business name, email, phone number, business address.
  • Business data: trading status (sole trader / company), company number (where applicable), VAT number (where applicable).
  • Verification documents: insurance certificates, qualification certificates, canine first aid certification, photo ID (reviewed and not retained beyond verification — see the Groomer Verification Policy).
  • Service data: listings, prices, opening hours, availability, services offered.
  • Payout data: bank details (held by Stripe, not Groomr), tax information, payout history.
  • Performance data: bookings, completion rates, cancellations, reviews, response times.

3.3 Data collected automatically from everyone using the Platform

  • Device and technical data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device identifiers, time zone.
  • Usage data: pages visited, features used, search queries, click paths, time spent on pages, referring URL.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: see our Cookie Policy for the full list and your choices.

3.4 Data we receive from third parties

  • Stripe: payment confirmations, payout statuses, KYC verification status (for Groomers), and dispute or chargeback notifications.
  • Clerk: authentication events (sign-in, sign-out, multi-factor authentication outcomes).
  • Google Maps: address-to-coordinate conversions when you enter a postcode or address.
  • Cloudinary: metadata about images you upload, such as file size and format.

3.5 Special-category and criminal-offence data

We do not seek special-category data (such as health, religion, sexual orientation) about Owners or Groomers as part of normal use of the Platform. We do not seek criminal-offence data. If you provide any such data voluntarily (for example, by mentioning a disability in a message), we will treat it with appropriate care and only use it for the purpose you supplied it for, or with your explicit consent.

4. How and Why We Use Your Personal Data

Under UK GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for each use of your personal data. The table below summarises what we do, why we do it, and which lawful basis applies.

4.1 To provide the Platform and process bookings

What: creating and managing your account; matching Owners with Groomers; processing bookings, payments and refunds; handling cancellations.

Lawful basis: performance of a contract with you (UK GDPR Article 6(1)(b)).

4.2 To verify Groomers

What: reviewing insurance, qualification, and identity documents under the Groomer Verification Policy.

Lawful basis: performance of the Groomer contract with you; our legitimate interests in maintaining a safe marketplace; and, for photo ID, our legitimate interests in fraud prevention. ID documents are inspected and discarded immediately upon completion of the verification.

4.3 To handle disputes and complaints

What: reviewing booking history, messages, and reviews to mediate disputes between Owners and Groomers; addressing welfare or safety concerns.

Lawful basis: performance of contract; our legitimate interests in operating a fair and safe marketplace; compliance with legal obligations.

4.4 To communicate with you

What: transactional emails (booking confirmations, reminders, receipts, account notifications); responses to your queries; service announcements.

Lawful basis: performance of contract; our legitimate interests in keeping you informed about your bookings and the Platform.

4.5 To send marketing communications

What: newsletters, product updates, special offers, and partner promotions.

Lawful basis: your consent (UK GDPR Article 6(1)(a) and PECR Regulation 22), which you can withdraw at any time by clicking unsubscribe in any marketing email, by changing your preferences in your account, or by emailing privacy@groomr.uk. We will not send you marketing communications without your prior opt-in.

4.6 To improve the Platform

What: analysing how the Platform is used; identifying bugs and pain points; building new features; conducting product research.

Lawful basis: our legitimate interests in improving and developing the Platform. Where we use cookies or similar technologies for this purpose, we will rely on your consent under PECR — see our Cookie Policy.

4.7 To prevent fraud and abuse, and to protect users

What: monitoring for fraudulent payments; investigating suspected misuse of the Platform; suspending accounts where necessary.

Lawful basis: our legitimate interests in protecting users and operating a safe marketplace; compliance with legal obligations.

4.8 To comply with legal obligations

What: accounting, tax, anti-money-laundering, responding to lawful requests from authorities, dealing with regulators.

Lawful basis: compliance with a legal obligation (UK GDPR Article 6(1)(c)).

4.9 If we ever change why we use your data

If we want to use your personal data for a new purpose that is not compatible with the purposes set out above, we will tell you and, where required, ask for your consent.

5. When We Rely on Legitimate Interests

Where we rely on "legitimate interests" as our lawful basis, we have considered whether our interests are outweighed by your interests, rights and freedoms. The main legitimate interests we rely on are: operating a safe and trustworthy marketplace; preventing fraud; protecting animal welfare; improving the Platform; communicating with you about your bookings; and pursuing the ordinary commercial interests of our business.

You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests — see section 9. If you do, we will stop the processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds, or unless the processing is necessary to establish, exercise or defend legal claims.

Groomers see the Owner's name, contact details, dog details, booking notes and any messages. Owners see the Groomer's name, business name, contact details, address (for in-salon services), reviews and services. Reviews are visible to all Platform users.

7. International Transfers

Most of the personal data we hold is stored within the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area. However, some of our service providers (notably Clerk, Stripe, Vercel, Cloudinary, Resend, Google and PostHog) may process personal data outside the UK and EEA, including in the United States.

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK, we rely on one of the following safeguards:

  • a UK Government "adequacy regulation" that recognises the destination country as offering an adequate level of data protection (for example, the UK–US Data Bridge for certified US recipients);
  • the International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) issued by the ICO; or
  • the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses.

You can request a copy of the relevant transfer safeguards by emailing privacy@groomr.uk.

8. How Long We Keep Your Personal Data

We keep personal data for only as long as we need it for the purpose we collected it for, including to satisfy any legal, accounting, tax, or reporting requirements. Our default retention periods are:

  • Account data: while your account is active, and for 12 months after closure (to handle disputes, refunds, and complaints), then deleted or anonymised.
  • Booking and transaction records: 7 years from the date of the booking, to comply with HMRC record-keeping requirements.
  • Payment data held by Stripe: retained by Stripe in accordance with their own policy and applicable financial regulation.
  • Identity documents (Groomer photo ID): inspected during verification and discarded immediately upon completion.
  • Verification certificates (insurance, qualifications): held while the Groomer is on the Platform, and for 3 years after their account is closed (to evidence verification at the time of past bookings).
  • Messages exchanged on the Platform: retained for the lifetime of the account; archived for 12 months after account closure.
  • Reviews: retained for the lifetime of the Groomer's account, even after the Owner closes their own account (the reviewer's name may be anonymised to "Former user" after closure).
  • Marketing preferences: kept while you have an account; we keep a suppression record indefinitely after opt-out so we do not contact you again.
  • Server logs and security logs: up to 12 months.
  • Analytics data: up to 24 months, or as set out in our Cookie Policy.

9. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have a number of rights in relation to your personal data. These are:

  • The right to be informed — about how we use your personal data (this Policy is part of how we meet that).
  • The right of access — you can ask for a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • The right to rectification — you can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data. Most personal data can be edited directly in your account settings.
  • The right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — you can ask us to delete personal data in certain circumstances. We may need to keep some data to comply with legal obligations (for example, tax records).
  • The right to restrict processing — you can ask us to limit how we use your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • The right to data portability — you can ask for a copy of personal data you have provided to us in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • The right to object — you can object to processing based on legitimate interests (including profiling), and you can object at any time to processing for direct marketing.
  • Rights relating to automated decision-making — we do not make decisions about you that have significant legal or similar effects based solely on automated processing.
  • The right to withdraw consent — where we rely on your consent (such as for marketing or non-essential cookies), you can withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

9.1 How to exercise your rights

Email privacy@groomr.uk. We will respond within one month. If your request is complex or you have made several requests, we may extend the deadline by a further two months and tell you why. There is no fee for exercising your rights in most cases; we may charge a reasonable fee or refuse a request that is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

We may need to ask you for information to confirm your identity before acting on a request. This is to protect your data from someone impersonating you.

9.2 Right to complain to the ICO

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, please contact us first so we can try to put it right. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office:

  • Website ico.org.uk
  • Helpline 0303 123 1113

11. How We Protect Your Personal Data

We take the security of personal data seriously. Among other measures, we:

  • encrypt data in transit using TLS;
  • encrypt sensitive data at rest where supported by our providers;
  • apply row-level security in our database so that users can access only the data they are entitled to;
  • require multi-factor authentication for our own administrative access;
  • delegate payment card processing to Stripe so that we never see or store full card numbers;
  • review our security practices periodically as the business grows;
  • vet our service providers for their own security and data protection practices.

If we become aware of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will report it to the ICO within 72 hours where required, and we will tell you without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to you.

12. Children

The Platform is not intended for use by anyone under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you become aware that a child has provided personal data to us, please contact privacy@groomr.uk so we can delete it.
